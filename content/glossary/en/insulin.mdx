## What It Is

Insulin is a hormone made by the pancreas that helps glucose enter cells for energy. In type 1 diabetes the body makes little or no insulin; in type 2 diabetes cells resist insulin's effects. People with diabetes may need insulin injections or other medicines to control blood sugar.

## Why It Matters

Insulin timing and dosing errors can cause dangerous highs and lows within hours. Understanding how your body uses insulin helps you coordinate meals, activity, and sick-day rules.

## Talk With Your Care Team

Ask: When should I take each insulin type relative to meals? What sick-day adjustments apply, and what blood sugar levels mean I need emergency help?
