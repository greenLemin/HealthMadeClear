## What It Is

Hypertension is the medical term for high blood pressure — when blood pushes too hard against artery walls over time. It is often called a "silent killer" because it may cause no symptoms while damaging the heart, brain, kidneys, and eyes. Treatment includes lifestyle changes and often medication.

## Why It Matters

Untreated hypertension damages blood vessels silently for years. Home monitoring and medication adherence directly lower stroke, heart attack, and kidney failure risk.

## Talk With Your Care Team

Ask: What blood pressure goal should I work toward, and how often should I check at home? What side effects from my blood pressure medicine should I report?
