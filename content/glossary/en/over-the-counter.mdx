## What It Is

Over-the-counter (OTC) medicines can be bought without a prescription — examples include acetaminophen, ibuprofen, and allergy pills. They still have risks and drug interactions. Tell your doctor and pharmacist about all OTC products you use, including how often and at what dose.

## Why It Matters

OTC products are real medicines with overdose and interaction risks — especially acetaminophen hidden in cold formulas. They belong on your full medication list for every visit.

## Talk With Your Care Team

Ask: Is this OTC product safe with my prescriptions and health conditions? What is the maximum daily dose from all sources combined?
