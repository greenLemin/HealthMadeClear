## What It Is

A sign is an objective finding that others can observe or measure — such as a fever, rash, or high blood pressure reading. Signs differ from symptoms, which are what the patient feels and reports. Doctors use both signs and symptoms to make diagnoses.

## Why It Matters

Signs give objective evidence doctors measure — fever, blood pressure, lab flags. Knowing the difference from symptoms helps you describe both clearly at visits.

## Talk With Your Care Team

Ask: What signs will you monitor at follow-up, and can I track any of them at home? Which new signs should prompt a call before my next appointment?
