## What It Is

Cholesterol is a waxy fat-like substance found in your blood. Your body needs some cholesterol to build cells, but too much LDL ("bad") cholesterol can build up in arteries and raise heart attack and stroke risk. HDL ("good") cholesterol helps remove excess cholesterol from the bloodstream.

## Why It Matters

Cholesterol levels help estimate cardiovascular risk alongside blood pressure, smoking, and family history. Lifestyle and medicines target LDL and triglycerides to protect arteries over decades.

## Talk With Your Care Team

Ask: Which numbers on my lipid panel matter most for me? When should we repeat the test after starting diet changes or statins?
