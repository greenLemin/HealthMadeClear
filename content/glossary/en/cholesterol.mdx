## What It Is

Cholesterol is a waxy fat-like substance found in your blood. Your body needs some cholesterol to build cells, but too much LDL ("bad") cholesterol can build up in arteries and raise heart attack and stroke risk. HDL ("good") cholesterol helps remove excess cholesterol from the bloodstream.

## Why It Matters

Understanding this term helps you follow conversations with doctors, read labels and test results, and make informed decisions about your health.

## Talk With Your Care Team

If you are unsure how this applies to you, write down your questions before appointments and ask for explanations in plain language.
