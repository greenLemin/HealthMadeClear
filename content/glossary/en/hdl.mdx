## What It Is

HDL (high-density lipoprotein) is often called "good" cholesterol because it carries cholesterol away from arteries back to the liver. Higher HDL levels are generally associated with lower heart disease risk. Exercise, not smoking, and healthy fats can help raise HDL.

## Why It Matters

HDL helps remove cholesterol from arteries, but it is one piece of heart risk — not a standalone guarantee of protection. Very low HDL may prompt lifestyle changes even when LDL looks acceptable.

## Talk With Your Care Team

Ask: Is my HDL level a concern given my overall heart risk? What lifestyle changes could raise HDL, and when would medicine be considered?
