## What It Is

Acute means sudden in onset and often short in duration. Acute illnesses or injuries develop quickly and may need prompt care — for example a broken bone, strep throat, or a panic attack. Acute is the opposite of chronic, which lasts months or years.

## Why It Matters

Knowing whether a problem is acute or chronic helps you set expectations for recovery time and urgency of follow-up. Acute symptoms that worsen quickly may need same-day care rather than waiting for a routine appointment.

## Talk With Your Care Team

Ask: Is this condition likely to resolve on its own, and what warning signs mean I should call sooner? How long should I wait before expecting improvement?
