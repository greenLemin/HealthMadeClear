## Question 1

What is a health insurance premium?

A) The amount you pay when you visit the doctor
B) The monthly payment you make to keep your insurance active
C) The discount you get for using in-network providers
D) The total amount your employer pays for your healthcare

answer: B
explanation: A premium is the amount you pay each month to have health insurance coverage. You pay this whether or not you use medical services. Premiums vary based on the plan type, coverage level, and your age or location.

## Question 2

What is a deductible in health insurance?

A) The amount you pay each time you fill a prescription
B) The amount you must pay out of pocket before your insurance starts covering services
C) The monthly payment to keep your insurance
D) The maximum amount your insurance will pay in a year

answer: B
explanation: A deductible is the amount you pay for covered healthcare services before your insurance plan starts to pay. For example, if your deductible is 1,000 dollars, you pay the first 1,000 dollars of covered services yourself. Some preventive services are covered before you meet the deductible.

## Question 3

What does "in-network" mean?

A) Doctors and hospitals that have a contract with your insurance plan to provide care at lower rates
B) Any doctor or hospital you choose to visit
C) A special network of doctors who only treat emergencies
D) Online doctors who you can only see through a computer

answer: A
explanation: In-network providers have agreed to accept your insurance plans negotiated rates. Using in-network providers usually costs you much less than going out-of-network. Always check if your doctor is in-network before scheduling to avoid surprise bills.

## Question 4

What is a copay?

A) A fixed amount you pay for a specific service, like 20 dollars for a doctors visit
B) The total cost of your medical treatment
C) A discount you receive for paying your premium early
D) The amount your insurance pays to the hospital

answer: A
explanation: A copay is a fixed dollar amount you pay at the time of service, such as 20 dollars for a primary care visit or 10 dollars for a prescription. Copays are separate from deductibles and usually do not count toward the deductible.

## Question 5

What is an out-of-pocket maximum?

A) The amount you can save by using insurance
B) The most you will have to pay for covered services in a plan year
C) The amount you owe if you cancel your insurance
D) The maximum amount your insurance pays for a single procedure

answer: B
explanation: The out-of-pocket maximum is the most you will pay for covered healthcare services in a year. Once you reach this limit, your insurance pays 100 percent of covered services for the rest of the plan year. This protects you from catastrophic medical costs.
