## Question 1

After a doctors visit, when should you fill a new prescription?

A) Only when you run out of your current medications
B) As soon as possible, ideally the same day
C) Whenever you remember, even if it is months later
D) Only if the pharmacy offers a discount

answer: B
explanation: Filling your prescription right away ensures you do not forget and start your treatment on schedule. It also lets the pharmacist check for drug interactions and answer any questions you may have.

## Question 2

What should you do if you have questions about your visit summary or discharge instructions?

A) Ignore them and hope for the best
B) Call your doctors office to clarify before the next appointment
C) Wait until your next visit and ask then
D) Ask a friend to interpret the medical terms

answer: B
explanation: If anything in your visit summary is unclear, call your doctors office soon after the visit. Getting clarification quickly helps you follow your care plan correctly and avoid mistakes with medications or follow-up steps.

## Question 3

Why is it important to schedule your next follow-up visit before leaving the office?

A) It saves you a phone call later and ensures you get an appointment at a good time
B) The office charges less if you schedule ahead of time
C) It is not important; you can call whenever you want
D) The doctor will only see you if you schedule before leaving

answer: A
explanation: Scheduling your follow-up before leaving guarantees you a convenient appointment time and reduces the chance you will forget to book one later. Keeping follow-up appointments is key to managing your health.

## Question 4

What should you do with medical documents like lab orders or referral forms after your visit?

A) Throw them away since everything is electronic now
B) Keep them in a folder or file where you can find them easily
C) Mail them back to your doctors office
D) Post them on the refrigerator as a reminder

answer: B
explanation: Keep important medical documents, lab orders, and referral forms in a designated folder or file. Having them organized and accessible ensures you can follow through with tests and specialist appointments without delay.

## Question 5

If your doctor ordered lab work, when should you expect to hear about the results?

A) The lab calls you the same day
B) Doctors usually contact patients within a few days to a week with results
C) You never hear back unless something is wrong
D) Results are mailed to your home address in one month

answer: B
explanation: Most doctors offices contact patients with lab results within a few days to a week. If you have not heard back after a week, call the office. No news is not always good news, so it is best to follow up.
