## Question 1

Which of the following symptoms requires immediate emergency care?

A) A mild headache after a long day
B) Trouble breathing or shortness of breath that does not go away
C) A small paper cut on your finger
D) A stuffy nose from seasonal allergies

answer: B
explanation: Trouble breathing is a medical emergency that requires immediate attention. Call 911 or go to the emergency room if you have difficulty breathing, chest pain, or sudden confusion. Do not wait to see if it gets better on its own.

## Question 2

What should you do if you have a fever of 103 degrees Fahrenheit or higher?

A) Take a cold shower immediately
B) Wait one week to see if it goes down
C) Call your doctor or seek medical attention
D) Cover yourself with heavy blankets

answer: C
explanation: A fever of 103 degrees Fahrenheit or higher in adults requires medical attention. High fevers can indicate serious infections. Contact your doctor or seek medical care, especially if the fever is accompanied by other symptoms like stiff neck or confusion.

## Question 3

What is a sign that chest pain might be serious and require emergency care?

A) The pain goes away when you change position
B) The pain is sharp and lasts only a second
C) The pain is accompanied by shortness of breath, nausea, or pain spreading to the arm or jaw
D) The pain happens only when you take a deep breath

answer: C
explanation: Chest pain accompanied by shortness of breath, nausea, lightheadedness, or pain spreading to the arm, back, or jaw could be a heart attack. Call 911 immediately. Do not drive yourself to the hospital.

## Question 4

Which of the following sudden symptoms could indicate a stroke?

A) A mild muscle cramp in your leg
B) Numbness or weakness on one side of the face or body, confusion, or trouble speaking
C) A headache that goes away after drinking water
D) Blurred vision that clears up after blinking

answer: B
explanation: Use the FAST acronym to recognize stroke: Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call 911. Sudden numbness, confusion, trouble speaking, or vision changes require immediate emergency care.

## Question 5

If you cut yourself and the bleeding does not stop after 10 minutes of direct pressure, what should you do?

A) Keep applying pressure and seek medical help
B) Pour rubbing alcohol on the wound
C) Wait another hour to see if it stops
D) Remove the bandage and let the wound air dry

answer: A
explanation: If bleeding does not stop after 10 to 15 minutes of steady direct pressure, seek medical attention. You may need stitches or other treatment to close the wound and prevent excessive blood loss.
