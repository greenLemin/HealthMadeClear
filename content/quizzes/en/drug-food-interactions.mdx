## Question 1

Why can grapefruit juice be problematic with certain medicines?

A) It always makes medicines work faster safely
B) It can inhibit enzymes and raise drug blood levels
C) It only affects vitamins, not prescriptions
D) It has no clinical significance

answer: B
explanation: Grapefruit inhibits intestinal CYP3A4, increasing absorption of some drugs to potentially toxic levels. It does not universally speed drugs safely, affects many prescriptions, and has well-documented clinical effects.

## Question 2

Why might levothyroxine be taken on an empty stomach?

A) Food always improves thyroid absorption
B) Food and minerals like calcium can reduce absorption
C) It must always be taken with a high-fat meal
D) Timing never matters for thyroid medicine

answer: B
explanation: Food, calcium, and iron can interfere with levothyroxine absorption, so consistent fasting timing is often recommended. Food does not reliably improve absorption; high-fat meals are not required; timing does matter.

## Question 3

What dietary approach helps people on warfarin?

A) Completely avoid all vitamin K forever
B) Keep vitamin K intake fairly consistent day to day
C) Double leafy greens weekly for health
D) Stop warfarin when eating salad

answer: B
explanation: Warfarin dosing is adjusted to consistent vitamin K intake — sudden large changes matter more than moderate steady amounts. Avoiding all vitamin K is unnecessary; erratic doubling or stopping warfarin around meals is unsafe.

## Question 4

Why limit alcohol with acetaminophen?

A) Alcohol improves liver detoxification of acetaminophen
B) Both can stress the liver and increase injury risk
C) Alcohol has no liver effects
D) Only children are at risk

answer: B
explanation: Alcohol and acetaminophen both burden hepatic pathways; combined use raises liver injury risk. Alcohol does not protect the liver, does have hepatic effects, and adults are also at risk.

## Question 5

What should you do when starting a medicine with food warnings on the label?

A) Ignore the label if you feel fine
B) Follow label directions and ask a pharmacist if unclear
C) Always take every drug with a full meal
D) Only take medicines with grapefruit juice

answer: B
explanation: Labels reflect how food affects that specific drug. Ignoring guidance risks under- or overdosing; not every drug needs food; grapefruit is contraindicated for many medicines, not a universal aid.
