## Question 1

What is the first thing you should look at on a nutrition facts label?

A) The brand name
B) The serving size and the number of servings per container
C) The expiration date
D) The ingredient list in small font

answer: B
explanation: Serving size is the foundation of the nutrition label. All the information on the label is based on one serving. If you eat two servings, you need to double all the numbers. Many packages that look like single servings actually contain multiple servings.

## Question 2

What does the "% Daily Value" tell you on a nutrition label?

A) The exact number of calories you should eat each day
B) How much a nutrient in one serving contributes to a daily diet based on a 2,000-calorie diet
C) The percentage of the food that is fat
D) The amount of sugar you should eat per day

answer: B
explanation: Percent Daily Value shows how much a nutrient in one serving contributes to a total daily diet. A DV of 5 percent or less is low, and 20 percent or more is high. Use this to quickly compare foods and see if they are high or low in specific nutrients.

## Question 3

Which of the following nutrients should you try to limit according to the nutrition label?

A) Dietary fiber
B) Vitamin D
C) Saturated fat, sodium, and added sugars
D) Potassium

answer: C
explanation: Health experts recommend limiting saturated fat, sodium, and added sugars because they are linked to heart disease, high blood pressure, and other health problems. Look for low DV percentages for these nutrients. Fiber, vitamins, and minerals are beneficial.

## Question 4

What is the difference between "total sugars" and "added sugars" on a label?

A) They are the same thing
B) Total sugars includes natural and added sugars; added sugars shows how much sugar was added during processing
C) Added sugars only includes artificial sweeteners
D) Total sugars does not include sugar from fruit

answer: B
explanation: Total sugars includes both naturally occurring sugars found in foods like fruit and dairy plus any sugars added during processing. Added sugars specifically shows how much sugar was added. The American Heart Association recommends limiting added sugars.

## Question 5

Why is the ingredient list important on a food package?

A) It shows the brand name
B) Ingredients are listed in descending order by weight, so the first ingredients make up the most of the food
C) It tells you when the food was manufactured
D) It lists the price per ingredient

answer: B
explanation: Ingredients are listed from the most to the least by weight. If sugar or a type of oil is listed first, the product is mostly made of that ingredient. This helps you identify hidden sources of sugar, unhealthy fats, and artificial additives.
