## Question 1

What are the main components of blood?

A) Water, salt, and sugar
B) Red blood cells, white blood cells, platelets, and plasma
C) Oxygen, carbon dioxide, and nitrogen
D) Vitamins, minerals, and protein

answer: B
explanation: Blood is made of four main components. Red blood cells carry oxygen, white blood cells fight infection, platelets help with clotting, and plasma is the liquid that carries them all. Each plays a vital role in keeping you healthy.

## Question 2

What is the main function of red blood cells?

A) Fighting infections
B) Carrying oxygen from the lungs to the rest of the body
C) Helping blood clot after an injury
D) Digesting food in the stomach

answer: B
explanation: Red blood cells contain hemoglobin, a protein that picks up oxygen in your lungs and delivers it to tissues throughout your body. Low red blood cell counts can cause fatigue and weakness, a condition called anemia.

## Question 3

What do white blood cells do?

A) Help you breathe
B) Carry nutrients to your organs
C) Fight infections and protect against illness
D) Control your body temperature

answer: C
explanation: White blood cells are part of your immune system. They identify and attack germs like bacteria and viruses. A high white blood cell count can indicate your body is fighting an infection.

## Question 4

What is the role of platelets in the blood?

A) Carrying oxygen to the brain
B) Fighting viruses
C) Helping blood clot to stop bleeding
D) Transporting hormones

answer: C
explanation: Platelets are tiny cell fragments that clump together at the site of an injury to form a plug and stop bleeding. Without enough platelets, even small cuts can bleed excessively.

## Question 5

What is plasma?

A) The solid part of blood
B) The liquid part of blood that carries cells, nutrients, and waste
C) A type of white blood cell
D) A protein that helps with digestion

answer: B
explanation: Plasma is the liquid portion of blood, making up about 55 percent of total blood volume. It carries blood cells, nutrients, hormones, and waste products throughout the body. Plasma is mostly water but contains important proteins and electrolytes.
