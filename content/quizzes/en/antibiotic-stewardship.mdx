## Question 1

Antibiotics are effective against which type of infection?

A) Most common colds caused by viruses
B) Bacterial infections when appropriately prescribed
C) All sore throats regardless of cause
D) Seasonal allergies

answer: B
explanation: Antibiotics target bacteria, not viruses or allergies. Most colds and many sore throats are viral; only specific bacterial causes warrant antibiotics after proper evaluation.

## Question 2

Why is stopping antibiotics early a concern?

A) It always causes instant resistance in the patient
B) It may not fully clear the infection and can select harder-to-treat bacteria
C) It makes the medicine work better later
D) It has no effect on treatment outcomes

answer: B
explanation: Incomplete treatment can leave surviving bacteria and contribute to resistance. It does not instantly make you permanently resistant, does not improve future efficacy, and clearly affects outcomes.

## Question 3

What is a serious side effect linked to antibiotic use?

A) C. difficile colitis
B) Improved vision
C) Permanent immunity to all infections
D) Lower cholesterol

answer: A
explanation: Antibiotics can disrupt gut flora and allow C. diff overgrowth, causing severe diarrhea. They do not improve vision, grant broad immunity, or lower cholesterol.

## Question 4

What should you do with leftover antibiotic pills?

A) Save them for the next cold
B) Share with a family member with similar symptoms
C) Dispose of them safely per pharmacy guidance — do not self-treat later
D) Take double dose to finish the bottle faster

answer: C
explanation: Leftover antibiotics may be the wrong drug, dose, or duration for a new illness. Saving, sharing, or doubling doses without medical direction is unsafe.

## Question 5

Why does the CDC emphasize antibiotic stewardship?

A) Antibiotics are no longer used in hospitals
B) Resistant bacteria make infections harder and sometimes impossible to treat
C) Viruses are becoming resistant to antibiotics
D) Stewardship means avoiding all medical care

answer: B
explanation: Resistance limits treatment options for serious bacterial infections. Antibiotics remain essential in hospitals; viruses do not become antibiotic-resistant — bacteria do; stewardship promotes appropriate use, not avoiding care.
