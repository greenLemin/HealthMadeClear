## Question 1

What is preventive care?

A) Treatment you receive after getting sick
B) Medical care focused on preventing illness or detecting it early before it becomes serious
C) Emergency care for life-threatening conditions
D) Alternative medicine that replaces regular doctor visits

answer: B
explanation: Preventive care includes check-ups, vaccinations, and screenings that help prevent disease or catch it early when it is easiest to treat. Regular preventive care can help you live a longer, healthier life.

## Question 2

Why are regular check-ups important even if you feel healthy?

A) They are not important if you feel fine
B) Some health problems have no symptoms in early stages and can only be detected through screenings
C) They are only required for people over age 65
D) They are mainly for getting free samples from the doctor

answer: B
explanation: Many serious conditions like high blood pressure, high cholesterol, and some cancers have no early symptoms. Regular check-ups and screenings can detect these problems early when treatment is most effective.

## Question 3

Under the Affordable Care Act, which preventive services are typically covered at no cost?

A) Only emergency room visits
B) Many preventive services like vaccines and cancer screenings are covered without a copay
C) No services are covered without cost
D) Only prescription medications

answer: B
explanation: The Affordable Care Act requires most insurance plans to cover recommended preventive services like annual check-ups, vaccines, and cancer screenings without charging a copay or deductible. This makes it easier for people to get preventive care.

## Question 4

How often should most adults have a routine physical exam?

A) Every 10 years
B) Only when they are sick
C) Once a year or as recommended by their doctor
D) Never, if they feel healthy

answer: C
explanation: Most adults should have an annual physical exam. Your doctor may recommend a different schedule based on your age, health conditions, and risk factors. Regular visits help track changes in your health over time.

## Question 5

What is a screening test?

A) A test that diagnoses a disease you already have symptoms for
B) A test that looks for disease in people who do not have symptoms yet
C) A test that determines your blood type
D) A test that measures your fitness level

answer: B
explanation: Screening tests are given to people without symptoms to detect potential health problems early. Examples include mammograms for breast cancer, colonoscopies for colon cancer, and blood pressure checks for hypertension.
