## Question 1

What is hypertension?

A) Low blood pressure that causes dizziness
B) Chronically high blood pressure that puts strain on the heart and blood vessels
C) A condition where blood pressure goes up and down rapidly
D) A temporary increase in blood pressure during exercise

answer: B
explanation: Hypertension means your blood pressure stays higher than normal over time. This constant strain damages arteries and increases the risk of heart attack, stroke, and kidney disease. It is often called the silent killer because it has no symptoms.

## Question 2

Which lifestyle change can help lower blood pressure?

A) Eating more salty foods
B) Reducing sodium intake, eating a balanced diet, and getting regular exercise
C) Drinking more caffeine
D) Sleeping fewer than 5 hours per night

answer: B
explanation: Reducing salt, eating more fruits and vegetables, maintaining a healthy weight, regular physical activity, limiting alcohol, and managing stress can all help lower blood pressure. Even small changes can make a meaningful difference.

## Question 3

What is a normal blood pressure reading?

A) 140 over 90 or higher
B) 130 over 80 or higher
C) Below 120 over 80
D) 150 over 100

answer: C
explanation: A normal blood pressure reading is below 120 systolic and below 80 diastolic. Readings of 130 over 80 or higher are considered elevated or stage 1 hypertension and should be discussed with your doctor.

## Question 4

Why is medication often needed to manage hypertension?

A) Lifestyle changes never work for lowering blood pressure
B) Some people need medication because lifestyle changes alone are not enough to control their blood pressure
C) Medication cures hypertension permanently
D) Medication is only needed for people over 80

answer: B
explanation: While lifestyle changes are important, many people with hypertension also need medication to reach a safe blood pressure level. Medications work in different ways to relax blood vessels or reduce fluid volume. Never stop blood pressure medication without talking to your doctor.

## Question 5

What should you do if you miss a dose of your blood pressure medication?

A) Take two doses together when you remember
B) Skip the dose and do not worry about it
C) Follow the instructions on your prescription label or ask your pharmacist
D) Stop taking all medications until you see your doctor

answer: C
explanation: If you miss a dose, check your prescription label or call your pharmacist. Some medications should be taken as soon as you remember, while others should be skipped if it is close to the next dose. Never double up unless instructed.
