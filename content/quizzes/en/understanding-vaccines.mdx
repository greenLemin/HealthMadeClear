## Question 1

How do vaccines work to protect you from disease?

A) They cure diseases after you get sick
B) They train your immune system to recognize and fight specific germs
C) They create a barrier on your skin that blocks germs
D) They replace your bodys natural immune system

answer: B
explanation: Vaccines contain weakened or inactivated parts of a germ. Your immune system learns to recognize the germ and builds antibodies against it. If you are later exposed to the real germ, your body can fight it off quickly.

## Question 2

What is herd immunity?

A) A type of vaccine given to farm animals
B) When most of a population is vaccinated, making it harder for disease to spread
C) Immunity that is passed down from parents to children
D) Natural immunity from getting sick

answer: B
explanation: Herd immunity occurs when a high percentage of a community is vaccinated, creating a barrier that slows or stops disease spread. This protects people who cannot get vaccinated, such as those with weakened immune systems or allergies.

## Question 3

Why are booster shots sometimes needed?

A) Vaccines expire like food does
B) Immunity from some vaccines can fade over time, and boosters strengthen the protection
C) Boosters are optional and do not provide additional protection
D) Boosters are only for children, never for adults

answer: B
explanation: For some vaccines, immunity decreases over time. A booster shot reminds your immune system to produce more antibodies, keeping you protected. Examples include tetanus boosters every 10 years and COVID-19 boosters.

## Question 4

Which of the following is true about vaccine side effects?

A) Vaccines cause the disease they are meant to prevent in most people
B) Most side effects are mild and short-lived, like soreness at the injection site or low fever
C) Vaccines have no side effects at all
D) Side effects are always severe and dangerous

answer: B
explanation: Common vaccine side effects include soreness, redness at the injection site, and mild fever. These are normal signs that your immune system is responding. Serious side effects are extremely rare compared to the risks of the diseases vaccines prevent.

## Question 5

Which vaccine is recommended annually for most people?

A) The tetanus vaccine
B) The flu (influenza) vaccine
C) The hepatitis B vaccine
D) The polio vaccine

answer: B
explanation: The flu vaccine is recommended every year because flu viruses change over time. Each years vaccine is updated to match the strains expected to be most common. Annual vaccination gives you the best protection against the current flu season.
