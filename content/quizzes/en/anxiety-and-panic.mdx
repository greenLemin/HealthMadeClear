## Question 1

What is the difference between normal anxiety and an anxiety disorder?

A) There is no difference
B) Normal anxiety is temporary and proportional to a situation; an anxiety disorder involves excessive, persistent worry that interferes with daily life
C) Anxiety disorders are caused by bad parenting
D) Normal anxiety only happens at night

answer: B
explanation: Everyone feels anxious sometimes. Normal anxiety is a natural response to stress and goes away when the situation passes. An anxiety disorder involves intense, excessive worry that lasts for months and makes it hard to work, sleep, or socialize.

## Question 2

What is a panic attack?

A) A sudden, intense wave of fear that triggers severe physical reactions even when there is no real danger
B) A mild feeling of nervousness before a test
C) A chronic state of worry that lasts all day
D) A type of allergic reaction

answer: A
explanation: Panic attacks come on suddenly and can include racing heart, chest pain, sweating, shaking, shortness of breath, and a feeling of losing control. Many people mistake panic attacks for heart attacks. They usually peak within 10 minutes.

## Question 3

Which of the following is a common symptom of generalized anxiety disorder?

A) Feeling perfectly calm in all situations
B) Excessive worry about everyday things like work, health, or family that is hard to control
C) Complete inability to feel fear
D) Sudden bursts of uncontrollable laughter

answer: B
explanation: People with generalized anxiety disorder worry excessively about a variety of everyday concerns. The worry is out of proportion to the situation, difficult to control, and often accompanied by restlessness, fatigue, muscle tension, and trouble sleeping.

## Question 4

What breathing technique can help during a moment of acute anxiety?

A) Holding your breath for as long as possible
B) Taking fast, shallow breaths
C) Slow, deep breathing where you breathe in for 4 counts, hold for 4, and breathe out for 4
D) Breathing only through your mouth rapidly

answer: C
explanation: Slow, deep breathing activates the parasympathetic nervous system, which helps calm the bodys stress response. Box breathing in for 4, hold for 4, out for 4 is a simple technique that can reduce anxiety symptoms in the moment.

## Question 5

Is anxiety treatable?

A) No, anxiety is a lifelong sentence
B) Yes, anxiety disorders are highly treatable with therapy, medication, or both
C) Yes, but only with medication that has severe side effects
D) No, anxiety can only be managed with alcohol

answer: B
explanation: Anxiety disorders are very treatable. Cognitive behavioral therapy is particularly effective. Many people also benefit from medication. With proper treatment, most people with anxiety disorders can manage their symptoms and live full, productive lives.
