## Question 1

Which of the following is the most important reason to tell your doctor about all medications you are taking?

A) So the doctor can prescribe more of the same medication
B) To avoid dangerous drug interactions between medications
C) To save money on your pharmacy bill
D) So the doctor can contact your pharmacist

answer: B
explanation: Telling your doctor about every medication you take, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements, helps prevent harmful drug interactions. Some medications can cancel each other out or combine to cause serious side effects.

## Question 2

What should you ask your doctor before starting a new medication?

A) What color the pill is
B) Whether similar medications are cheaper in other countries
C) What the medication is for, how to take it, and what side effects to expect
D) How much weight you will lose from taking it

answer: C
explanation: Before starting any new medication, you should understand its purpose, the correct dosage and timing, potential side effects, and any food or activity restrictions. This helps you take the medication safely and effectively.

## Question 3

What does the term "generic" mean when referring to medication?

A) A medication that is less effective than the brand name
B) A medication that does not require a prescription
C) A copy of a brand-name drug that has the same active ingredients
D) A medication that is only available in liquid form

answer: C
explanation: Generic medications contain the same active ingredients as brand-name drugs and must meet the same FDA standards for safety and effectiveness. They are usually much cheaper than brand-name versions.

## Question 4

If you forget to take a dose of your medication, what is the safest approach?

A) Take a double dose at the next scheduled time
B) Skip the missed dose and never take it again
C) Check the medication label or ask your pharmacist what to do
D) Crush the pill and mix it with food

answer: C
explanation: Instructions for missed doses vary by medication. Some should be taken as soon as you remember, while others should be skipped if close to the next dose. Always check the label or ask your pharmacist to be safe.

## Question 5

Why is it important to keep an updated list of your medications with you at all times?

A) So you can show your insurance company
B) Emergency responders need to know what medications you take
C) To prove you are following your doctors orders
D) So you can compare prices at different pharmacies

answer: B
explanation: In an emergency, paramedics and emergency room doctors need to know exactly what medications you are taking to make safe treatment decisions. An updated list can prevent dangerous errors.
