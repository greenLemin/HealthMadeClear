## Question 1

Which of the following is a reason to call 911?

A) You have a mild cold and want to see a doctor
B) Someone is unconscious, not breathing, or having chest pain
C) You need a ride to the pharmacy
D) Your internet service is not working

answer: B
explanation: Call 911 for any life-threatening emergency: difficulty breathing, chest pain, signs of stroke, severe bleeding, choking, unconsciousness, or someone who stops breathing. When in doubt, call. Dispatchers can guide you on what to do while help arrives.

## Question 2

What should you do first when calling 911?

A) Hang up and call your doctor instead
B) Stay as calm as possible and clearly tell the dispatcher your location and what is happening
C) Start performing first aid before calling
D) Look up the nearest hospital address

answer: B
explanation: Stay calm and give the dispatcher your exact location and a clear description of the emergency. Answer their questions. They are trained to help you and will give instructions. Do not hang up until the dispatcher tells you to.

## Question 3

If someone is choking but is coughing forcefully, what should you do?

A) Perform the Heimlich maneuver immediately
B) Encourage them to keep coughing and stay with them
C) Call 911 right away
D） Give them water to drink

answer: B
explanation: If a choking person is coughing forcefully, their airway is partially blocked and they may be able to clear it on their own. Stay with them and encourage them to keep coughing. Only perform abdominal thrusts if they cannot cough, speak, or breathe.

## Question 4

When should you NOT call 911?

A) When someone is having a severe allergic reaction
B) When you have a minor cut that you can treat with a bandage at home
C) When you witness a car accident with injuries
D) When someone is having a stroke

answer: B
explanation: 911 is for emergencies that threaten life, limb, or property. Minor injuries that can be treated at home, routine medical questions, or non-urgent transportation needs should be handled through your primary care doctor or urgent care.

## Question 5

What information should you have ready when calling 911?

A) The patients favorite color
B) Your exact location, what happened, how many people are hurt, and the patients condition
C) The patients social security number
D) The patients blood type

answer: B
explanation: The dispatcher needs to know where to send help, what kind of emergency it is, and how many people are involved. Knowing the patients condition (conscious, breathing, bleeding) helps dispatchers send the right resources and give you instructions.
