## Question 1

What does "Rx" on a prescription label mean?

A) Remedy X-ray
B) Prescription (from Latin _recipere_)
C) Required eXtract
D) Repeat dosage

answer: B
explanation: "Rx" comes from the Latin word _recipere_, meaning "to take." It is the standard abbreviation for a medical prescription.

## Question 2

Which part of the prescription label tells you how often to take your medication?

A) Patient name
B) Pharmacy name
C) Dosage instructions
D) Refill count

answer: C
explanation: The dosage instructions (or sig) tell you how much to take, how often, and by which route. Patient and pharmacy details are identifiers, and refill count shows remaining authorizations.

## Question 3

What does "NDC" stand for on a prescription label?

A) National Drug Code
B) New Dosage Calculation
C) Named Drug Classification
D) Non-Drowsy Compound

answer: A
explanation: The NDC is a unique identifier for the medication, including the manufacturer, product, and package size. It helps prevent dispensing errors.

## Question 4

If your label says "Take 1 tablet by mouth twice daily," when should you take it?

A) Every 2 hours
B) Once in the morning and once at night
C) Only when symptoms appear
D) Every 12 hours exactly

answer: B
explanation: "Twice daily" usually means morning and evening, approximately 10-12 hours apart. Taking with meals can help you remember.

## Question 5

What should you do if the medication name on the label does not match what your doctor said?

A) Take it anyway
B) Call your pharmacist before taking it
C) Look it up online
D) Wait until your next appointment

answer: B
explanation: Always verify with your pharmacist if the medication name looks different from what you expected. The generic name may differ from the brand name, but it is safest to confirm.

## Question 6

Why is the expiration date important on a prescription label?

A) It is optional
B) The medication may be less effective or unsafe after this date
C) It tells you when to refill
D) It shows when the pharmacy opened the bottle

answer: B
explanation: Expired medications can lose potency or degrade into harmful compounds. Always check the expiration date and properly dispose of expired medicines.

## Question 7

What do the warning labels on a prescription bottle indicate?

A) The cost of the medication
B) Special precautions like drowsiness or avoiding alcohol
C) The manufacturer's address
D) How to refill

answer: B
explanation: Warning labels highlight specific safety precautions such as "may cause drowsiness," "avoid alcohol," or "take with food." Always read these before starting a new medication.

## Question 8

What information is NOT typically found on a prescription label?

A) Prescribing doctor's name
B) Pharmacy phone number
C) Medication cost
D) Refill information

answer: C
explanation: Prescription labels include the patient name, doctor name, pharmacy details, medication name, dosage, instructions, and refill info — but not the cost. The pharmacy can provide pricing separately.

## Question 9

If your label says "May cause drowsiness," what should you avoid?

A) Drinking water
B) Driving or operating heavy machinery
C) Eating food
D) Taking other medications

answer: B
explanation: Drowsiness warnings mean the medication can impair your alertness. Avoid driving or operating heavy machinery until you know how the medicine affects you.

## Question 10

What does the refill number on a label tell you?

A) How many times you can refill the prescription
B) When the medication expires
C) The pharmacy's refill policy
D) The doctor's DEA number

answer: A
explanation: The refill count shows how many additional times you can get the same prescription refilled without seeing your doctor. "Refills: 0" means you need a new prescription.
