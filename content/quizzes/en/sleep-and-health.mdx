## Question 1

How many hours of sleep do most adults need per night?

A) 4 to 5 hours
B) 7 to 9 hours
C) 10 to 12 hours
D) 5 to 6 hours

answer: B
explanation: Most adults need 7 to 9 hours of quality sleep per night for optimal health. Consistent sleep helps your body repair, supports brain function, and strengthens your immune system. Sleeping too little over time increases health risks.

## Question 2

What is sleep hygiene?

A) Washing your bedsheets every night
B) Habits and practices that promote good, consistent sleep
C) A special diet for people who cannot sleep
D) Using medication to fall asleep faster

answer: B
explanation: Sleep hygiene refers to healthy sleep habits like keeping a consistent bedtime, avoiding screens before bed, limiting caffeine in the evening, and creating a cool, dark, quiet sleep environment. Good sleep hygiene helps you fall asleep and stay asleep.

## Question 3

How does chronic lack of sleep affect your health?

A) It has no effect on health
B) It increases the risk of obesity, heart disease, diabetes, and weakened immune function
C) It only affects your mood, not your physical health
D) It improves memory and concentration

answer: B
explanation: Chronic sleep deprivation is linked to serious health problems including obesity, heart disease, high blood pressure, diabetes, and depression. It also weakens the immune system, making you more likely to get sick.

## Question 4

What is sleep apnea?

A) A condition where you cannot fall asleep at night
B) A disorder where breathing repeatedly stops and starts during sleep
C) A condition where you sleep too much
D) A temporary phase of insomnia

answer: B
explanation: Sleep apnea causes repeated pauses in breathing during sleep, which reduces oxygen levels and disrupts sleep quality. Common signs include loud snoring, gasping for air during sleep, and daytime fatigue. It can increase the risk of heart problems if untreated.

## Question 5

What should you avoid before bedtime to improve sleep?

A) Reading a book
B) Using phones, tablets, or computers because blue light can interfere with your body's sleep signals
C) Drinking herbal tea
D) Taking a warm bath

answer: B
explanation: Blue light from screens suppresses melatonin, the hormone that signals your body to sleep. Avoiding screens for at least 30 to 60 minutes before bed, along with limiting caffeine and heavy meals, helps you fall asleep more easily.
