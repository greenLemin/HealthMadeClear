## Question 1

What happens in the body of someone with type 2 diabetes?

A) The body produces too much insulin
B) The body becomes resistant to insulin or does not produce enough insulin to control blood sugar
C) The body cannot digest carbohydrates at all
D) The body stops producing red blood cells

answer: B
explanation: In type 2 diabetes, your cells become resistant to insulin, a hormone that helps sugar enter your cells for energy. Over time, the pancreas cannot produce enough insulin to keep blood sugar levels normal. This leads to high blood sugar.

## Question 2

What is a common symptom of uncontrolled high blood sugar?

A) Weight gain and slow heart rate
B) Frequent urination, excessive thirst, and unexplained weight loss
C) Improved vision and hearing
D) Decreased appetite and difficulty sleeping

answer: B
explanation: When blood sugar is high, your kidneys work overtime to remove excess sugar through urine. This causes frequent urination and thirst. Other symptoms include fatigue, blurry vision, and slow-healing cuts. These symptoms develop gradually in type 2 diabetes.

## Question 3

How can diet help manage type 2 diabetes?

A) Eating only one meal per day
B) Avoiding all carbohydrates completely
C) Eating balanced meals with controlled portions, limiting sugar and refined carbs
D) Drinking only fruit juice

answer: C
explanation: A diabetes-friendly diet focuses on portion control, fiber-rich foods, lean protein, and healthy fats. Monitoring carbohydrate intake helps keep blood sugar stable. A registered dietitian can help create a personalized meal plan.

## Question 4

What is the A1C test?

A) A daily blood sugar test done with a finger stick
B) A blood test that measures average blood sugar levels over the past 2 to 3 months
C) A test for kidney function
D) A urine test for ketones

answer: B
explanation: The A1C test provides an average of your blood sugar levels over the past 2 to 3 months. It shows how well your diabetes is being managed. Most people with diabetes should have an A1C test at least twice a year.

## Question 5

What foot care practice is important for people with diabetes?

A) Walking barefoot to strengthen the feet
B) Checking feet daily for cuts, blisters, or sores
C) Soaking feet in hot water every night
D) Applying ice to the feet before bed

answer: B
explanation: Diabetes can reduce blood flow and sensation in the feet, making it hard to feel injuries. Small cuts can become infected and lead to serious complications. Daily foot checks help catch problems early before they become severe.
