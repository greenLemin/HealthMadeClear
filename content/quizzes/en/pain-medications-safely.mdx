## Question 1

What organ is most at risk from too much acetaminophen?

A) Heart
B) Liver
C) Kidneys
D) Lungs

answer: B
explanation: Acetaminophen is processed by the liver. Taking more than the daily limit — especially from multiple products — can cause serious liver damage. The heart, kidneys, and lungs are not the primary organs at risk from acetaminophen overdose.

## Question 2

Why check cold medicine labels for acetaminophen?

A) It is always safe to combine
B) Multiple products can exceed daily limits
C) It only matters for children
D) Labels are optional

answer: B
explanation: Many cold, flu, and pain products contain acetaminophen under different brand names. Adding them together can push you past the safe daily limit without realizing it. Always read active ingredients on every label.

## Question 3

When should you avoid ibuprofen without medical advice?

A) Mild headache
B) Known kidney disease or stomach ulcers
C) After exercise
D) When food is available

answer: B
explanation: NSAIDs like ibuprofen can worsen kidney function and irritate the stomach lining. People with kidney disease, stomach ulcers, or on blood thinners should only use them with medical guidance. A mild headache in someone without those conditions is different.

## Question 4

What should you do for chest pain?

A) Take extra pain medicine and wait
B) Seek emergency care immediately
C) Only use ice packs
D) Ignore it if you are young

answer: B
explanation: Chest pain can signal a heart attack or other life-threatening condition. Pain medicine will not fix the underlying emergency. Call 911 or go to the emergency room immediately — do not wait to see if it passes.

## Question 5

Why tell your pharmacist all medicines you take?

A) To check interactions and duplications
B) Pharmacies sell more products
C) It is not necessary for OTC drugs
D) Only for prescriptions from one doctor

answer: A
explanation: Pharmacists check for duplicate ingredients (like acetaminophen in multiple products) and dangerous drug interactions. This is especially important when you take prescriptions, OTC medicines, and supplements together.
