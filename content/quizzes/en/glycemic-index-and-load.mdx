## Question 1

What does glycemic load (GL) add beyond glycemic index (GI)?

A) It ignores portion size entirely
B) It accounts for both food GI and serving size
C) It only measures protein content
D) It replaces blood glucose monitoring

answer: B
explanation: GL multiplies GI by available carbohydrate amount per serving, making portion size part of the estimate. GL does not ignore portions, measure protein, or replace actual glucose monitoring.

## Question 2

Which meal strategy tends to blunt glucose spikes?

A) Carbohydrates alone in large portions
B) Adding fiber, protein, or healthy fat to carbohydrate foods
C) Skipping all vegetables
D) Only drinking sugary beverages

answer: B
explanation: Fiber, protein, and fat slow carbohydrate absorption and moderate post-meal glucose. Large carbs alone, avoiding vegetables, or sugary drinks tend to raise glucose more sharply.

## Question 3

How does the ADA generally view GI/GI-only diets?

A) As the sole treatment for diabetes
B) As one tool within broader healthy eating patterns
C) As unnecessary for everyone with diabetes
D) As a cure for type 1 diabetes

answer: B
explanation: ADA guidance emphasizes overall dietary patterns, medications, and activity — GI/GL can inform choices but are not standalone treatment or cures, and they remain useful for many with diabetes.

## Question 4

Which statement about sugar and type 2 diabetes is most accurate?

A) Eating sugar alone directly causes type 2 diabetes in everyone
B) Type 2 risk involves genetics, weight, activity, and metabolism — not sugar alone
C) Only children develop type 2 diabetes
D) Sugar has no effect on blood glucose

answer: B
explanation: Type 2 diabetes is multifactorial; sugar affects glucose but does not singularly cause the disease in everyone. Adults develop type 2 commonly; sugar clearly affects blood glucose.

## Question 5

Why might two people respond differently to the same high-GI food?

A) GI is identical for every human always
B) Individual metabolism, activity, and medications vary
C) GI only applies to fats
D) Blood glucose never changes after meals

answer: B
explanation: Real-world glucose responses differ by insulin sensitivity, activity, gut microbiome, and meds. GI varies by food, not only fats, and post-meal glucose clearly changes.
