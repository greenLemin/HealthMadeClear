## Question 1

According to the MyPlate model, what portions of your plate should be filled with fruits and vegetables?

A) One quarter
B) About half of the plate
C) The entire plate
D) A small corner of the plate

answer: B
explanation: The MyPlate model recommends filling half your plate with fruits and vegetables. These foods are rich in vitamins, minerals, and fiber while being low in calories. Eating a variety of colorful produce ensures you get a range of nutrients.

## Question 2

What type of grains should make up at least half of the grains you eat?

A) Refined white grains like white bread and white rice
B) Whole grains like whole wheat, brown rice, oats, and quinoa
C) Enriched grains only
D) Grain-based desserts like cookies and cake

answer: B
explanation: Whole grains contain the entire grain kernel, providing more fiber, protein, vitamins, and minerals than refined grains. Fiber helps with digestion, keeps you full longer, and can lower cholesterol and blood sugar levels.

## Question 3

What is a healthy source of protein to include on your plate?

A) Fried chicken and bacon
B) Lean meat, poultry, fish, beans, eggs, nuts, and tofu
C) Processed lunch meats
D) Foods fried in oil

answer: B
explanation: Healthy protein sources include lean meats, fish, poultry, eggs, beans, lentils, tofu, nuts, and seeds. These provide essential amino acids without excess saturated fat. Aim for a variety of protein sources throughout the week.

## Question 4

What type of fat is considered heart-healthy?

A) Saturated fats found in butter and red meat
B) Trans fats found in many processed snacks
C) Unsaturated fats found in olive oil, avocados, nuts, and fish
D) All fats are equally healthy

answer: C
explanation: Unsaturated fats, including monounsaturated and polyunsaturated fats, can help improve cholesterol levels and reduce heart disease risk. Sources include olive oil, avocados, nuts, seeds, and fatty fish like salmon. Limit saturated and avoid trans fats.

## Question 5

What should you drink with a balanced meal for the healthiest choice?

A) Sugary soda or sweetened tea
B) Water or unsweetened beverages
C) Energy drinks
D) Fruit punch with added sugar

answer: B
explanation: Water is the healthiest choice to accompany any meal. It hydrates without adding calories or sugar. Sugary drinks are a major source of added sugar and empty calories. Unsweetened tea or milk are also good options.
