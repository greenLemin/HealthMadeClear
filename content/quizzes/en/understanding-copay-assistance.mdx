## Question 1

What is a common goal of manufacturer copay cards?

A) Replace insurance entirely
B) Lower patient out-of-pocket cost for a specific drug
C) Increase drug prices
D) Eliminate need for prescriptions

answer: B
explanation: Manufacturer copay cards reduce what you pay at the pharmacy for a specific brand drug. They do not replace insurance or eliminate the need for a valid prescription — they lower your out-of-pocket share for eligible patients.

## Question 2

What should you do before stopping a medicine you cannot afford?

A) Stop immediately
B) Talk with your doctor about alternatives and assistance
C) Buy from unknown online sellers only
D) Double the dose to make it last

answer: B
explanation: Stopping a needed medicine suddenly can be dangerous. Your doctor may know about generics, alternative doses, samples, or assistance programs. Always discuss affordability before changing or stopping treatment.

## Question 3

Why be cautious with unsolicited copay help calls?

A) They are always legitimate
B) Scams may ask for sensitive personal information
C) Pharmacies never offer real programs
D) Insurance forbids all assistance

answer: B
explanation: Legitimate programs use official websites and clinic financial counselors. Scammers may ask for your Social Security number, bank details, or payment upfront. Never share sensitive information with unsolicited callers.

## Question 4

Who can help navigate assistance at many hospitals?

A) Only the billing department after discharge
B) Financial counselor or social worker
C) No one — you must figure it out alone
D) Only drug sales representatives

answer: B
explanation: Hospital financial counselors and social workers know manufacturer programs, nonprofit grants, and local resources. They can help you apply without navigating complex rules alone — ask at registration or billing.

## Question 5

Why re-check assistance eligibility yearly?

A) Programs never change
B) Insurance, income rules, and program funding can change
C) Once approved, it lasts forever
D) Doctors handle this automatically

answer: B
explanation: Assistance programs depend on insurance type, income limits, and manufacturer funding. A change in your job, Medicare status, or plan design can end eligibility. Re-check each year and when your coverage changes.
