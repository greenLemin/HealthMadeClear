## Question 1

What is anaphylaxis?

A) A mild seasonal allergy
B) A severe, potentially life-threatening allergic reaction
C) A type of food intolerance
D) A skin rash that always goes away on its own

answer: B
explanation: Anaphylaxis is a severe allergic reaction that can affect breathing and blood pressure and requires emergency treatment.

## Question 2

What should you do first if you have epinephrine and signs of anaphylaxis?

A) Wait to see if symptoms improve
B) Use epinephrine as directed and call 911
C) Take only an antihistamine
D) Drive yourself to urgent care

answer: B
explanation: Epinephrine is first-line emergency treatment for anaphylaxis. Call 911 because a second wave of symptoms can occur.

## Question 3

Why tell every doctor and pharmacist about your allergies?

A) It is only required for food allergies
B) To avoid medicines or products that could trigger a reaction
C) Pharmacies already have this information automatically
D) It is only needed for children

answer: B
explanation: Drug and latex allergies are common. Providers need your full allergy history to prescribe and dispense safely.

## Question 4

Which is an example of an environmental allergy trigger?

A) Penicillin
B) Pollen
C) Shellfish
D) Latex gloves only

answer: B
explanation: Pollen is a common environmental allergen. Medicines, foods, and latex are other categories of triggers.

## Question 5

Why is self-diagnosing food allergies risky?

A) All food allergies are mild
B) Eliminating foods without testing can cause nutritional gaps and miss other conditions
C) Doctors cannot test for food allergies
D) Food labels list all allergens in every country

answer: B
explanation: Professional evaluation helps confirm true allergies and safe diets. Unnecessary restriction can harm nutrition.
