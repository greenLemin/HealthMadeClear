## Question 1

What does an H flag usually mean?

A) Healthy
B) High relative to reference range
C) Hospital only
D) Hormone test only

answer: B
explanation: High relative to reference range — correct.

## Question 2

Reference ranges:

A) Are identical in every lab worldwide
B) Can vary by lab and patient factors
C) Replace medical advice
D) Mean you definitely have disease if outside

answer: B
explanation: Can vary by lab and patient factors — correct.

## Question 3

Why compare past results?

A) Never useful
B) Trends help show if values are changing over time
C) Only for children
D) Portals do this illegally

answer: B
explanation: Trends help show if values are changing over time — correct.

## Question 4

Slightly abnormal result — best first step?

A) Panic
B) Discuss with your doctor in context
C) Change dose yourself
D) Ignore forever

answer: B
explanation: Discuss with your doctor in context — correct.

## Question 5

Critical value on portal with no call — you should:

A) Wait a month
B) Contact your care team promptly
C) Post online only
D) Retest at home only

answer: B
explanation: Contact your care team promptly — correct.
