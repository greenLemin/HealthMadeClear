## Question 1

What should you do if you do not understand something your doctor says during the appointment?

A) Nod and agree so you do not seem difficult
B) Ask the doctor to explain it in simpler terms
C) Look it up on your phone after the appointment
D) Assume it is not important

answer: B
explanation: It is your right to understand your health. Doctors use medical terms that can be confusing. Asking for clarification helps you make informed decisions about your care. A good doctor will be happy to explain.

## Question 2

Why is it important to be honest with your doctor about your habits like smoking, drinking, or diet?

A) So the doctor can judge your lifestyle choices
B) Because these habits affect your health and treatment options
C) So the information can be shared with your employer
D) It is not important; you can keep these private

answer: B
explanation: Your lifestyle directly affects your health risks and how well treatments will work. Doctors need accurate information to provide the best care. They are bound by privacy laws and are there to help, not judge.

## Question 3

What is a "telehealth" visit?

A) A visit where you talk to your doctor through a phone call or video call
B) A visit where a robot examines you
C) A visit where you send emails to your doctor
D) A visit that happens over the telephone only for emergencies

answer: A
explanation: Telehealth uses technology to connect you with your doctor remotely through phone or video. It is convenient for follow-ups, discussing test results, and managing ongoing conditions when an in-person exam is not needed.

## Question 4

If you see a medical student or a nurse practitioner during your visit, what should you do?

A) Refuse to speak with anyone except the main doctor
B) Ask for a discount because you are seeing a trainee
C) Treat them with respect and answer their questions honestly
D) Leave the appointment immediately

answer: C
explanation: Medical students and nurse practitioners are trained professionals working as part of your care team. They often spend more time with patients and communicate well. You can always ask for the attending doctor if you prefer.

## Question 5

What should you do before leaving your doctors appointment?

A) Ask for a copy of your medical records to take home
B) Make sure you understand the next steps: medications, follow-up visits, and any tests needed
C) Request a second opinion from every doctor in the office
D) Ask the receptionist to call you a taxi

answer: B
explanation: Before leaving, confirm your understanding of the treatment plan, including any new prescriptions, recommended tests, and when to schedule a follow-up. This prevents confusion and helps you follow through with your care.
