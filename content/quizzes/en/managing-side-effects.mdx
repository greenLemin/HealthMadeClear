## Question 1

What should you do first if you experience a side effect from a medication?

A) Immediately stop taking the medication
B) Contact your doctor or pharmacist to discuss the symptom
C) Double your dose to make the side effect go away faster
D) Wait one month to see if it gets worse

answer: B
explanation: Always contact your doctor or pharmacist when you notice a side effect. They can determine if it is normal, adjust your dose, or switch you to a different medication. Stopping medication suddenly can be dangerous.

## Question 2

Which of the following is a common side effect of many medications?

A) Improved vision
B) Increased height
C) Nausea or upset stomach
D) Enhanced memory

answer: C
explanation: Nausea and digestive upset are among the most common side effects across many different medications. Taking medication with food or at a specific time of day can often help reduce these symptoms.

## Question 3

What is the difference between a side effect and an allergic reaction?

A) There is no difference, they are the same thing
B) A side effect is expected and often mild; an allergic reaction involves the immune system and can be serious
C) An allergic reaction only happens with generic drugs
D) Side effects only happen with liquid medications

answer: B
explanation: Side effects are known possible reactions that are usually mild and may go away as your body adjusts. Allergic reactions involve the immune system and can cause hives, swelling, or trouble breathing, which requires immediate medical attention.

## Question 4

What can help reduce medication side effects like dizziness or drowsiness?

A) Taking the medication with grapefruit juice
B) Taking the medication at bedtime if your doctor approves
C) Chewing the medication instead of swallowing it
D) Drinking alcohol to counteract the dizziness

answer: B
explanation: Taking certain medications at bedtime can help manage side effects like dizziness or drowsiness. Always ask your doctor or pharmacist about the best time to take your medication and whether food or specific timing can help.

## Question 5

If you are taking multiple medications and notice new symptoms, what could be happening?

A) The symptoms are definitely unrelated to your medications
B) You may be experiencing a drug interaction between medications
C) You should take extra vitamins to fix the symptoms
D) Your body is building immunity to the medications

answer: B
explanation: New symptoms when taking multiple medications could signal a drug interaction. Certain combinations can change how medications work in your body. Always report new symptoms to your doctor, especially when starting a new medication.
