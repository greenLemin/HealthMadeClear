## Question 1

What causes type 1 diabetes?

A) Autoimmune destruction of insulin-producing pancreatic cells
B) Eating too much candy alone in every person
C) Only obesity in adults over 65
D) Lack of exercise for one week

answer: A
explanation: Type 1 is immune-mediated beta cell loss requiring insulin. Candy alone does not cause type 1; obesity is central to type 2 risk, not type 1 mechanism; brief inactivity does not explain type 1.

## Question 2

Which treatment is essential from diagnosis for type 1 diabetes?

A) Insulin therapy
B) Only herbal supplements
C) Avoiding all carbohydrates forever
D) No monitoring needed

answer: A
explanation: Type 1 always needs insulin for survival. Supplements cannot replace insulin; zero-carb diets are not standard mandatory therapy; monitoring is essential for safety.

## Question 3

What best describes type 2 diabetes pathophysiology?

A) Insulin resistance with progressive insulin secretory decline
B) Complete absence of any insulin from birth in all cases
C) Only a viral infection lasting two days
D) Exclusively a childhood autoimmune disease

answer: A
explanation: Type 2 combines resistance and relative insulin deficiency over time. Total absence from birth describes type 1; brief viruses and childhood autoimmunity do not define type 2.

## Question 4

Why is distinguishing type 1 from type 2 clinically important?

A) Treatment and urgency differ — insulin is lifesaving in type 1
B) Both are identical and need no insulin ever
C) Only type 2 requires any medical follow-up
D) Type 1 never causes emergencies

answer: A
explanation: Misclassification risks DKA if type 1 is untreated with insulin. Types differ; both need follow-up; type 1 can cause rapid emergencies without insulin.

## Question 5

Which statement about sugar and diabetes is most accurate?

A) Sugar alone directly causes all diabetes types
B) Type 2 risk is multifactorial; type 1 is not caused by sugar intake alone
C) Only people who never eat sugar get diabetes
D) Sugar has no effect on blood glucose in any person

answer: B
explanation: Sugar affects glucose levels but does not singularly cause type 1 or all type 2. Avoiding sugar entirely does not guarantee prevention; sugar clearly impacts blood glucose.
