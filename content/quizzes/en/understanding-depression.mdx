## Question 1

What is depression?

A) The same as feeling sad for a few hours after a bad day
B) A common but serious mood disorder that affects how you feel, think, and handle daily activities
C) A sign of personal weakness
D) An illness that only affects older adults

answer: B
explanation: Depression is a medical condition, not a character flaw or temporary sadness. It involves persistent feelings of sadness, loss of interest in activities, and changes in sleep, appetite, and energy. It affects people of all ages and backgrounds.

## Question 2

Which of the following is a common symptom of depression?

A) Feeling unusually happy and energetic for weeks
B) Persistent sadness, loss of interest in hobbies, and changes in sleep or appetite
C) Sudden improvement in memory and concentration
D) Increased desire to socialize with others

answer: B
explanation: Common depression symptoms include persistent sadness or emptiness, loss of interest in activities once enjoyed, significant changes in weight or appetite, trouble sleeping or sleeping too much, low energy, difficulty concentrating, and thoughts of death or suicide.

## Question 3

How is depression typically treated?

A) Depression does not need treatment; it goes away on its own
B) Through therapy, medication, or a combination of both
C) Only by taking herbal supplements
D) By exercising for 12 hours a day

answer: B
explanation: Depression is highly treatable. The most effective approaches include psychotherapy or talk therapy, antidepressant medication, or both. Lifestyle changes like regular exercise and social support also help. What works best varies from person to person.

## Question 4

If someone says they are thinking about suicide, what should you do?

A) Ignore it because people who talk about suicide rarely mean it
B) Take it seriously, stay with them, and help them contact a crisis helpline or emergency services
C) Tell them to think about something else
D) Wait to see if they mention it again

answer: B
explanation: Any mention of suicide should be taken seriously. Stay with the person and call or text 988 (the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline) or 911. Do not leave them alone. Talking about suicide does not increase the risk; it provides an opportunity to help.

## Question 5

Can depression affect physical health?

A) No, depression is only in your mind
B) Yes, depression can cause physical symptoms like headaches, digestive problems, and chronic pain
C) No, mental health and physical health are completely separate
D) Yes, but only in people over 60

answer: B
explanation: Depression affects both mind and body. It can cause fatigue, headaches, back pain, digestive issues, and worsen chronic conditions like heart disease or diabetes. Treating depression often improves both mental and physical health.
