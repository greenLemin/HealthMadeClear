## Question 1

What does a CBC primarily measure?

A) Blood cells including RBCs, WBCs, and platelets
B) Cholesterol and triglycerides only
C) Kidney function creatinine only
D) Thyroid hormone TSH only

answer: A
explanation: CBC counts cellular blood components. Lipid panels, creatinine, and TSH are separate tests — not components of a standard CBC.

## Question 2

Low hemoglobin on a CBC most often suggests which condition?

A) Anemia
B) High blood sugar diabetes
C) Broken bone
D) Seasonal allergy

answer: A
explanation: Hemoglobin carries oxygen; low values define anemia. Diabetes, fractures, and allergies are not directly diagnosed by low hemoglobin alone on CBC.

## Question 3

Why might white blood cell count be elevated?

A) Infection, inflammation, or physiological stress
B) Only because of drinking water
C) Always means leukemia with no other causes
D) WBC never changes in illness

answer: A
explanation: Leukocytosis commonly follows infection and inflammation. Hydration alone does not typically spike WBC; leukemia is one rare cause among many; WBC clearly shifts with illness.

## Question 4

Why should you avoid starting iron supplements solely because of fatigue?

A) Anemia cause should be confirmed — excess iron can be harmful
B) Iron never treats anemia
C) CBC never detects low hemoglobin
D) Fatigue only comes from lack of sleep always

answer: A
explanation: Iron helps iron-deficiency anemia but harms if given unnecessarily. Iron does treat deficiency; CBC detects low hemoglobin; fatigue has many causes beyond sleep alone.

## Question 5

What do low platelets (thrombocytopenia) increase risk for?

A) Bleeding and bruising
B) Improved blood clotting always
C) Higher oxygen saturation automatically
D) Lower infection risk always

answer: A
explanation: Platelets enable clotting; low counts raise bleeding risk. Thrombocytopenia does not improve clotting, raise SpO2, or universally prevent infection.
