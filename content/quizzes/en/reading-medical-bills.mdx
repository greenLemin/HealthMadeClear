## Question 1

What should you check first when you receive a medical bill?

A) Whether the bill is printed on official letterhead
B) Whether your personal information, the dates of service, and the services listed are correct
C) Whether the bill has a watermark
D) Whether the envelope is sealed properly

answer: B
explanation: Always verify your name, the dates you received care, and the procedures listed. Mistakes in billing are common. An incorrect date, wrong procedure code, or mixed-up patient information can lead to being charged for services you did not receive.

## Question 2

What is an Explanation of Benefits or EOB?

A) A bill that shows how much you owe
B) A summary from your insurance company showing what services were billed, what was covered, and what you may owe
C) A letter explaining why your insurance was denied
D) A form you fill out to apply for insurance

answer: B
explanation: An EOB is not a bill. It is a statement from your insurance explaining how a claim was processed. It shows the amount billed, the insurance discount, what the insurance paid, and your remaining responsibility. Compare it with the providers bill to check for errors.

## Question 3

What does a "CPT code" on a medical bill represent?

A) The patients condition or diagnosis
B) A standard code that describes the medical procedure or service that was provided
C) The doctors license number
D) The hospitals room number

answer: B
explanation: CPT codes are standardized numbers assigned to every medical procedure and service, like a 99213 for an office visit. These codes determine how much you are charged and whether your insurance will cover the service. Errors in coding can lead to incorrect charges.

## Question 4

If you cannot afford to pay a medical bill, what should you do?

A) Ignore it and hope it goes away
B) Contact the billing office to ask about payment plans or financial assistance
C) Pay only half and assume that is enough
D) Dispute the entire bill without any reason

answer: B
explanation: Most hospitals and medical providers offer payment plans or charity care programs for patients who cannot pay their full bill. Contact the billing office and ask about options. Ignoring the bill can lead to collection actions and damage to your credit.

## Question 5

What is a common reason medical bills contain errors?

A) Hospitals intentionally overcharge all patients
B) Billing is complex and errors like duplicate charges, wrong codes, or incorrect insurance information are common
C) Doctors do not know how much services cost
D) Insurance companies refuse to share information

answer: B
explanation: Medical billing is complicated and error-prone. Common mistakes include duplicate charges for the same service, upcoding (billing for a more expensive service than was provided), and incorrect insurance information. Reviewing bills carefully can save you money.
