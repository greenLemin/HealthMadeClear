## Question 1

Obstructive sleep apnea is caused by:

A) Dreaming too much
B) Blocked airflow when throat muscles relax
C) Too much caffeine only
D) Normal snoring always

answer: B
explanation: Obstructive sleep apnea happens when throat muscles relax during sleep and block airflow. The brain briefly wakes you to breathe again. It is not simply snoring — it involves repeated breathing pauses.

## Question 2

A reason to seek evaluation:

A) Snoring plus daytime sleepiness
B) One night of poor sleep
C) Occasional yawn
D) Reading before bed

answer: A
explanation: Loud snoring plus daytime sleepiness, morning headaches, or a partner noticing breathing pauses are common warning signs. Snoring alone does not always mean apnea, but the combination warrants evaluation.

## Question 3

CPAP works by:

A) Surgery on the throat
B) Delivering air pressure to keep the airway open
C) Replacing all exercise
D) Stopping breathing on purpose

answer: B
explanation: CPAP delivers steady air pressure through a mask to keep the airway open during sleep. It is the most common effective treatment for moderate to severe obstructive sleep apnea — not a cure, but a nightly therapy.

## Question 4

Untreated sleep apnea increases risk of:

A) Only dry skin
B) High blood pressure and heart disease among others
C) Improved driving
D) Lower weight always

answer: B
explanation: Untreated sleep apnea is linked to high blood pressure, heart disease, stroke risk, and poor glucose control. Treating it protects more than sleep quality — it supports cardiovascular and metabolic health.

## Question 5

If CPAP is uncomfortable:

A) Stop forever without telling anyone
B) Ask a sleep specialist about mask fit and alternatives
C) Drive long trips while drowsy
D) Only use it once a month

answer: B
explanation: Mask fit, humidity settings, and alternative devices (oral appliances, positional therapy) can improve comfort. A sleep specialist can adjust settings — stopping CPAP without guidance usually brings symptoms back.
