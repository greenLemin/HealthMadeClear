## Question 1

How long do antidepressants often need before full benefit?

A) About 4 to 8 weeks for many people
B) Full effect within 2 hours always
C) At least 2 years before any change
D) Only one dose is ever needed

answer: A
explanation: Most antidepressants require weeks of consistent use. Two hours is unrealistic for full mood effect; two years is not a standard wait for initial response; chronic treatment may continue but not as a single dose.

## Question 2

What do SSRIs primarily affect?

A) Serotonin reuptake in the brain
B) Insulin production in the pancreas
C) Thyroid hormone synthesis only
D) Blood type antigens

answer: A
explanation: SSRIs selectively inhibit serotonin reuptake. They do not primarily act on insulin, thyroid synthesis, or blood group antigens.

## Question 3

Why monitor young adults when starting antidepressants?

A) Risk of worsening mood or suicidal thoughts in some patients early in treatment
B) Antidepressants always cause instant cure without side effects
C) Only children under age 2 take antidepressants
D) Monitoring is required because medicines are placebos only

answer: A
explanation: FDA black box warnings address possible suicidality monitoring early in treatment. Medicines are not instant cure-only drugs, are used in adolescents/adults not toddlers only, and are active drugs not placebos.

## Question 4

What can happen if antidepressants are stopped abruptly?

A) Discontinuation symptoms such as dizziness or flu-like feelings
B) Guaranteed permanent happiness
C) Instant cure of all anxiety forever
D) No possible effects whatsoever

answer: A
explanation: Abrupt stop can cause discontinuation syndrome. Permanent happiness, instant universal anxiety cure, and zero effects are not accurate outcomes of sudden stops.

## Question 5

Why tell all prescribers about antidepressants and supplements?

A) To avoid dangerous interactions like serotonin syndrome
B) So they can discourage all mental health care
C) Supplements never interact with prescriptions
D) Only one doctor ever needs medication information

answer: A
explanation: Combining serotonergic drugs and some supplements risks serotonin syndrome. Providers support care, supplements can interact, and all prescribers need complete med lists.
