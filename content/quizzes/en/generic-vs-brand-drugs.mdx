## Question 1

Generics must have the same:

A) Only color
B) Active ingredient and strength
C) Marketing name
D) Price as brand

answer: B
explanation: Active ingredient and strength — correct.

## Question 2

Why might insurance prefer generics?

A) They are less regulated
B) Lower cost with equivalent effectiveness for most drugs
C) They never work
D) They are always brands

answer: B
explanation: Lower cost with equivalent effectiveness for most drugs — correct.

## Question 3

After switching to generic, you should:

A) Stop all follow-up
B) Report new or worsening side effects to your care team
C) Double the dose
D) Never tell the pharmacist

answer: B
explanation: Report new or worsening side effects to your care team — correct.

## Question 4

Counterfeit drugs are a risk when:

A) Using licensed pharmacies
B) Buying from unverified online sellers
C) Reading labels
D) Asking questions

answer: B
explanation: Buying from unverified online sellers — correct.

## Question 5

Bioequivalent means:

A) No active drug
B) Similar blood levels compared to brand within accepted range
C) Only for vitamins
D) Illegal in the U.S.

answer: B
explanation: Similar blood levels compared to brand within accepted range — correct.
