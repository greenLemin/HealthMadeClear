## Question 1

What is asthma?

A) A condition where the lungs fill with fluid
B) A chronic condition where airways become inflamed and narrow, making it hard to breathe
C) A temporary infection of the throat
D) A type of allergic reaction to food

answer: B
explanation: Asthma is a chronic lung condition. The airways become swollen, produce extra mucus, and the muscles around them tighten. This narrows the airways and causes wheezing, coughing, chest tightness, and shortness of breath.

## Question 2

What is a common trigger for asthma symptoms?

A) Drinking cold water
B) Exercise, allergies, cold air, or respiratory infections
C) Eating too much protein
D) Exposure to sunlight

answer: B
explanation: Asthma triggers vary by person but commonly include pollen, dust mites, pet dander, mold, cold air, exercise, smoke, and colds or flu. Identifying and avoiding your personal triggers is a key part of asthma management.

## Question 3

What is the difference between a rescue inhaler and a controller inhaler?

A) There is no difference
B) Rescue inhalers are used daily; controller inhalers are used only in emergencies
C) Rescue inhalers provide quick relief during an asthma attack; controller inhalers reduce airway inflammation over time
D) Controller inhalers are only for children

answer: C
explanation: Rescue inhalers contain quick-acting medication that relaxes airway muscles during an attack, providing fast relief. Controller inhalers contain anti-inflammatory medication taken daily to prevent symptoms. Both play important roles in asthma management.

## Question 4

What should you do if your rescue inhaler does not relieve asthma symptoms?

A) Wait another hour and try again
B) Take a hot shower to open your airways
C) Seek emergency medical help immediately
D) Drink a large amount of water

answer: C
explanation: If your rescue inhaler does not work or symptoms get worse, seek emergency care right away. Severe asthma attacks can be life-threatening. Signs of a severe attack include gasping for air, inability to speak full sentences, and blue lips or fingernails.

## Question 5

Can asthma be cured?

A) Yes, with the right medication
B) No, asthma is a chronic condition that can be managed but not cured
C) Yes, but only with surgery
D) Yes, it goes away on its own after childhood

answer: B
explanation: Asthma is a chronic condition with no cure, but it can be effectively managed. With proper treatment, an asthma action plan, and avoiding triggers, most people with asthma can live active, full lives without frequent symptoms.
