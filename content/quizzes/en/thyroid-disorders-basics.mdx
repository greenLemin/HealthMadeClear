## Question 1

Which test is commonly used first to screen thyroid function?

A) TSH
B) Cholesterol only
C) Blood type
D) Skin biopsy

answer: A
explanation: TSH is the primary screening test for hypo- and hyperthyroidism. Cholesterol may rise in hypothyroidism but is not the screen; blood type and skin biopsy do not assess thyroid function.

## Question 2

Which symptoms align more with hypothyroidism?

A) Fatigue, weight gain, and cold intolerance
B) Only fever and rash for 24 hours
C) Sudden face droop and arm weakness
D) Improved marathon speed without training

answer: A
explanation: Hypothyroidism slows metabolism causing fatigue, weight gain, and cold sensitivity. Brief fever/rash, stroke signs, and unexplained athletic gains are not typical hypothyroid patterns.

## Question 3

What is a standard treatment for hypothyroidism?

A) Levothyroxine hormone replacement
B) High-dose iodine for everyone
C) Avoiding all medical follow-up
D) Only aspirin daily

answer: A
explanation: Levothyroxine replaces deficient T4. Iodine helps only in deficiency states and can worsen some conditions; follow-up is required; aspirin does not treat hypothyroidism.

## Question 4

Why can hyperthyroidism be dangerous if untreated?

A) It can cause rapid heart rate and serious arrhythmias
B) It always lowers heart rate to zero safely
C) It only affects hair color
D) It prevents all infections permanently

answer: A
explanation: Excess thyroid hormone stresses the cardiovascular system. It increases rather than safely zeroes heart rate; effects extend beyond cosmetic hair changes; it does not confer infection immunity.

## Question 5

Why tell the lab if you take biotin supplements before thyroid blood work?

A) Biotin can falsely affect some immunoassay thyroid results
B) Biotin is required for accurate TSH always
C) Labs refuse all patients on vitamins
D) Biotin cures Graves' disease

answer: A
explanation: High-dose biotin interferes with certain thyroid immunoassays. It is not required for accuracy; labs do not refuse vitamin users; biotin is not a Graves' cure.
