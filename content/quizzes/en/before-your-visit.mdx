## Question 1

What is the most important thing to bring to your doctors appointment?

A) A snack and a bottle of water
B) Your ID, insurance card, and a list of your current medications
C) A friend who can drive you home
D) Your favorite book to read in the waiting room

answer: B
explanation: Bringing your ID and insurance card is essential for check-in. A current medication list including doses and frequencies helps your doctor make safe and informed decisions about your care.

## Question 2

Why should you write down your questions before a doctors visit?

A) So you can impress your doctor with how prepared you are
B) To avoid forgetting important concerns during the appointment
C) Because the receptionist collects your questions at check-in
D) So the doctor does not have to talk to you

answer: B
explanation: Doctor visits are often short and can feel overwhelming. Writing down your questions beforehand ensures you cover everything that matters to you, even if you feel nervous or rushed during the appointment.

## Question 3

What should you do if you have new symptoms but your appointment is weeks away?

A) Wait until the appointment and then mention them
B) Call your doctors office to ask if you should come in sooner
C) Go to the emergency room for any new symptom
D) Try to treat the symptoms yourself without medical advice

answer: B
explanation: If new symptoms develop before your scheduled appointment, call your doctors office. They can advise whether you need to be seen sooner, speak with a nurse, or if it is safe to wait. Some symptoms need prompt attention.

## Question 4

How far in advance should you arrive for a typical doctors appointment?

A) 2 to 3 hours early
B) Exactly at the appointment time, not earlier
C) 10 to 15 minutes early to complete paperwork
D) Whenever you want, as long as you arrive before the office closes

answer: C
explanation: Arriving 10 to 15 minutes early gives you time to check in, update your insurance information, and complete any necessary forms. This helps the appointment start on time and reduces stress.

## Question 5

What is a "visit summary" and why is it important?

A) A summary of what you watched on TV in the waiting room
B) A document your doctor gives you that explains the visit, any new medications, and follow-up steps
C) A bill for the appointment
D) A summary your friend writes about your symptoms

answer: B
explanation: A visit summary is a document that outlines what was discussed during your appointment, including diagnoses, medication changes, and follow-up instructions. Reviewing it helps ensure you understand and remember your care plan.
