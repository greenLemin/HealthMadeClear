## Question 1

What is the primary mechanism of statins?

A) Blocking liver HMG-CoA reductase to reduce cholesterol production
B) Dissolving existing artery plaque overnight
C) Raising HDL by exercising muscles directly
D) Replacing all dietary cholesterol absorption

answer: A
explanation: Statins inhibit hepatic cholesterol synthesis, lowering LDL. They do not instantly dissolve plaque, do not directly exercise muscles, and do not block all dietary cholesterol uptake.

## Question 2

Why should muscle symptoms on a statin be reported?

A) They are always harmless and should be ignored
B) Rare severe muscle injury needs evaluation and possible medication change
C) They mean the statin is curing diabetes
D) Only athletes experience muscle effects

answer: B
explanation: Most muscle complaints are mild, but severe cases need assessment. Symptoms are not always harmless, do not indicate diabetes cure, and affect non-athletes too.

## Question 3

How do ACC/AHA guidelines generally position statins?

A) Only for people already hospitalized
B) For selected high-risk patients alongside lifestyle therapy
C) As replacements for blood pressure medicines
D) Only when LDL is normal

answer: B
explanation: Guidelines recommend statins based on risk and LDL in outpatient prevention too. They complement lifestyle, do not replace BP drugs, and are used when LDL or risk warrants treatment — not only at normal LDL.

## Question 4

What should you do before stopping a prescribed statin due to side effects?

A) Stop immediately without telling anyone
B) Discuss with your clinician for dose change or alternative
C) Double the dose to push through pain
D) Replace with unproven supplements only

answer: B
explanation: Clinicians can adjust therapy safely. Stopping without guidance risks cardiovascular events; doubling dose worsens toxicity; supplements alone lack proven event reduction.

## Question 5

Why might grapefruit be mentioned on some statin labels?

A) It improves statin safety for all statins equally
B) It can raise blood levels of certain statins via enzyme inhibition
C) It has no interaction with any medicines
D) It is required to absorb statins

answer: B
explanation: Grapefruit inhibits CYP3A4, increasing exposure for some statins like simvastatin and atorvastatin. It does not universally improve safety, does interact with drugs, and is not required for absorption.
