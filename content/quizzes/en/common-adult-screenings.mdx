## Question 1

At what age should most women begin getting mammograms to screen for breast cancer?

A) Age 30
B) Age 40 or as recommended by their doctor based on risk factors
C) Age 60
D) Age 21

answer: B
explanation: Most guidelines recommend women start regular mammograms at age 40. Women with a family history of breast cancer may need to start earlier. Mammograms can detect breast cancer years before a lump can be felt.

## Question 2

What is a colonoscopy used to screen for?

A) Stomach ulcers
B) Colon cancer and precancerous polyps
C) Kidney stones
D) Food allergies

answer: B
explanation: A colonoscopy allows doctors to examine the entire colon for abnormal growths called polyps. Removing these polyps can prevent colon cancer from developing. Screening usually begins at age 45 for average-risk adults.

## Question 3

What does a Pap smear screen for?

A) Ovarian cancer
B) Cervical cancer and precancerous changes in the cervix
C) Urinary tract infections
D) Thyroid problems

answer: B
explanation: A Pap smear collects cells from the cervix to look for abnormal changes that could lead to cervical cancer. Regular screening has dramatically reduced cervical cancer rates. Women typically start screening at age 21.

## Question 4

What is the PSA test used for?

A) Screening for prostate cancer in men
B) Testing for lung function
C) Diagnosing diabetes
D) Checking liver health

answer: A
explanation: The PSA blood test measures prostate-specific antigen, a protein produced by the prostate gland. High levels may indicate prostate cancer, though other conditions can also raise PSA. Men should discuss screening with their doctor starting around age 50.

## Question 5

Why are regular blood pressure screenings important as you age?

A) Blood pressure only matters if you have symptoms
B) High blood pressure often has no symptoms but can cause serious damage to your heart and arteries
C) Blood pressure screenings are only needed for athletes
D) Your blood pressure stays the same throughout your life

answer: B
explanation: High blood pressure is called the silent killer because it usually has no symptoms. Over time, it damages blood vessels and increases the risk of heart attack, stroke, and kidney disease. Regular screening is the only way to know your levels.
