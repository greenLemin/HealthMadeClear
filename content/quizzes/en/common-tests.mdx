## Question 1

What is a complete blood count or CBC test used for?

A) To measure how fast you can run
B) To check your overall health and detect disorders like anemia or infection
C) To see if your bones are broken
D) To test your eyesight

answer: B
explanation: A CBC measures red blood cells, white blood cells, hemoglobin, and platelets. It is a common screening test that can help detect anemia, infection, inflammation, and other blood disorders.

## Question 2

What does a blood pressure reading measure?

A) The speed of blood flow through your veins
B) The force of blood against the walls of your arteries when your heart beats and rests
C) The amount of sugar in your blood
D) How much oxygen is in your lungs

answer: B
explanation: Blood pressure has two numbers: systolic (pressure when the heart beats) and diastolic (pressure when the heart rests between beats). High blood pressure over time can damage your heart and arteries.

## Question 3

What is a cholesterol test also known as?

A) A stress test
B) A lipid panel or lipid profile
C) A glucose tolerance test
D) A thyroid panel

answer: B
explanation: A lipid panel measures total cholesterol, LDL (bad cholesterol), HDL (good cholesterol), and triglycerides. It helps assess your risk for heart disease and stroke.

## Question 4

What is the purpose of a urinalysis?

A) To test how much water you drink
B) To detect kidney problems, urinary tract infections, and other health conditions
C) To measure your lung capacity
D) To check your reflexes

answer: B
explanation: A urinalysis examines the color, concentration, and content of your urine. It can detect kidney disease, urinary tract infections, diabetes, and other conditions. It is a simple, non-invasive test.

## Question 5

What is a fasting blood glucose test used for?

A) To measure your blood sugar after eating a large meal
B) To screen for diabetes and prediabetes after not eating for at least 8 hours
C) To test your iron levels
D) To check your kidney function

answer: B
explanation: A fasting blood glucose test measures your blood sugar after an overnight fast. Normal levels are typically below 100 mg/dL. Higher levels may indicate prediabetes or diabetes and require follow-up testing.
