## Question 1

What is heart disease?

A) Any condition that affects the brain
B) A group of conditions that affect the heart and blood vessels, including coronary artery disease
C) An infection of the heart muscle
D) A temporary condition that only affects older adults

answer: B
explanation: Heart disease is an umbrella term for conditions affecting the heart and blood vessels. The most common type is coronary artery disease, where plaque builds up in the arteries supplying blood to the heart. Heart disease is the leading cause of death in the United States.

## Question 2

What is a heart attack?

A) When the heart stops beating completely
B) When blood flow to a part of the heart muscle is blocked, usually by a blood clot
C) When the heart beats too fast temporarily
D) When the lungs stop working

answer: B
explanation: A heart attack occurs when blood flow to the heart is severely reduced or blocked, usually by a clot in a narrowed artery. Without oxygen, heart muscle begins to die. Quick treatment is critical to minimize damage.

## Question 3

Which of the following is a major risk factor for heart disease?

A) Getting enough sleep
B) High blood pressure, high cholesterol, and smoking
C) Eating a balanced diet
D) Regular physical activity

answer: B
explanation: Major risk factors for heart disease include high blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, diabetes, obesity, and a family history of heart disease. Many of these can be managed through lifestyle changes and medication.

## Question 4

What lifestyle habit can reduce your risk of heart disease?

A) Smoking cigarettes in moderation
B) Eating a diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins
C) Drinking large amounts of alcohol every day
D) Sitting for most of the day without physical activity

answer: B
explanation: A heart-healthy diet includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats while limiting saturated fats, trans fats, sodium, and added sugars. Combined with regular exercise and not smoking, this can significantly reduce heart disease risk.

## Question 5

What does CPR stand for and what is its purpose?

A) Cardiac Pulse Regulation; it regulates the heart rhythm
B) Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation; it keeps blood and oxygen flowing during cardiac arrest
C) Cardio-Pulmonary Recovery; it helps after a heart attack
D) Cardiac Prevention Routine; it prevents heart disease

answer: B
explanation: CPR combines chest compressions and rescue breaths to keep blood and oxygen flowing to the brain and organs when a persons heart has stopped. Bystander CPR can double or triple a cardiac arrest victims chance of survival.
