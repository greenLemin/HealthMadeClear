## Why Blood Tests?

Blood tests help doctors understand what's happening inside your body. They can show:

- How well your organs are working
- If you have certain diseases or conditions
- How well medications are working
- If your immune system is fighting an infection

## Common Blood Tests

Some of the most common blood tests include:

- Complete Blood Count (CBC) - measures different parts of your blood
- Basic Metabolic Panel - checks kidney function, electrolytes, and blood sugar
- Lipid Panel - measures cholesterol and triglycerides
- Glucose Test - checks blood sugar levels for diabetes
- Thyroid Panel - checks how well your thyroid is working

## Understanding Reference Ranges

Blood test results show your numbers compared to a "reference range" - the range that's considered normal for most people. Your result might be:

- Within the normal range
- Above the normal range (high)
- Below the normal range (low)

Being slightly outside the range isn't always a problem. Your doctor will look at the whole picture.

:::info
Reference ranges can vary by lab, age, and sex. Always ask your doctor to explain your results.
:::
