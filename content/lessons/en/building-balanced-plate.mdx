## The Plate Method

The simplest way to build a healthy meal is the plate method. Divide your plate:

| Section             | Portion       | Examples                                                  |
| ------------------- | ------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| Vegetables + Fruits | **1/2 plate** | Salad, roasted broccoli, berries, bell peppers, carrots   |
| Lean Protein        | **1/4 plate** | Chicken breast, fish, tofu, beans, eggs, Greek yogurt     |
| Complex Carbs       | **1/4 plate** | Brown rice, quinoa, whole wheat pasta, sweet potato, oats |

Add: healthy fat (olive oil, avocado, nuts), water to drink.

## What Goes Where

### Vegetables and Fruits (Half Your Plate)

- **Go for color:** Different colors = different nutrients
  - **Green:** Broccoli, spinach, kale, zucchini — folate, vitamin K, fiber
  - **Red:** Tomatoes, red peppers, strawberries — lycopene, vitamin C
  - **Orange/Yellow:** Carrots, sweet potatoes, squash — beta-carotene, vitamin A
  - **Blue/Purple:** Blueberries, eggplant, purple cabbage — anthocyanins (antioxidants)
- **Fresh, frozen, or canned (no added salt/sugar) — all count**

### Lean Proteins (Quarter of Plate)

- **Animal options:** Chicken/turkey breast (skinless), fish, lean beef, eggs, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese
- **Plant options:** Beans (black, kidney, chickpea), lentils, tofu, tempeh, edamame, seitan
- **Fish twice a week:** Salmon, sardines, mackerel, trout (omega-3 fatty acids)

:::info
Plant proteins (beans, lentils) also count as vegetables AND protein. They're excellent sources of fiber too — aim for a few plant-based meals per week.
:::

### Complex Carbs (Quarter of Plate)

- **Choose whole grains:** Brown rice, quinoa, oats, barley, whole wheat bread, whole wheat pasta
- **Starchy vegetables:** Sweet potatoes, peas, corn, winter squash
- **Legumes:** Beans, lentils, chickpeas (can also be protein)

## Healthy Fats (Added to Meals)

| Fat          | Use                       | Nutrient                          |
| ------------ | ------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Olive oil    | Cooking, dressings        | Monounsaturated fat, antioxidants |
| Avocado      | Sandwiches, salads, toast | Fiber, potassium, healthy fat     |
| Nuts & seeds | Snacks, toppings          | Vitamin E, magnesium, healthy fat |
| Nut butters  | Sandwiches, oatmeal       | Protein, healthy fat              |

## What About Dairy?

- **Best choices:** Plain yogurt (Greek or regular), milk (cow or unsweetened plant-based), cottage cheese, cheese in moderation
- **Goal:** 2-3 servings/day for calcium and vitamin D
- **Watch:** Flavored yogurts are often high in added sugar. Choose plain.

## Sample Balanced Meals

### Breakfast

- **Oatmeal** (1/4 plate) with **berries** (1/2 plate) and **nuts** (protein/fat)
- **Scrambled eggs** (1/4) with **sautéed spinach and peppers** (1/2) and **whole wheat toast** (1/4)

### Lunch

- **Grilled chicken breast** (1/4) on a **large salad** (1/2) with **quinoa** (1/4) and **olive oil dressing** (fat)
- **Lentil soup** (protein + carbs) with a **side salad** (1/2 plate) and a **whole grain roll**

### Dinner

- **Baked salmon** (1/4) with **roasted broccoli** (1/2) and **brown rice** (1/4)
- **Stir-fry with tofu** (1/4), **mixed vegetables** (1/2), and **brown rice noodles** (1/4)

## Tips for Eating Well on a Budget

- **Frozen vegetables** are as nutritious as fresh and often cheaper
- **Canned beans** — rinse to reduce sodium by 40%
- **Buy in bulk** — rice, oats, beans, lentils
- **Seasonal produce** — cheaper and fresher
- **Frozen fruit** — good for smoothies, oatmeal, yogurt

## Foods to Limit

- **Sugary drinks** — soda, sweet tea, fruit punch, energy drinks (largest source of added sugar in American diet)
- **Ultra-processed foods** — packaged snacks, fast food, frozen meals, sugary cereals, processed meats (bacon, sausage, hot dogs)
- **Not a "never" list** — a balanced approach means less often, not never

:::success
A balanced plate doesn't have to be perfect. Start with one change: add one extra serving of vegetables to one meal per day.
:::

## Common Mistakes

1. **Portion distortion** — restaurant portions are often 2-3x actual serving sizes
2. **Skipping meals** — leads to overeating later
3. **Drinking too many calories** — soda, juice, sports drinks, sweetened coffee
4. **"Healthy" labels on packaged foods** — granola, protein bars, dried fruit can be high in sugar
5. **Not enough fiber** — most Americans get half the recommended amount

## Listen to Your Body

- **Eat when hungry** — don't wait until ravenous (you'll overeat)
- **Stop when comfortable** — not stuffed
- **Eat slowly** — it takes 20 minutes for your brain to register fullness
- **Don't eat distracted** — TV, phone, computer make you eat more
