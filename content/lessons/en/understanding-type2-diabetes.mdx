## What Is Type 2 Diabetes?

Type 2 diabetes means your body doesn't use insulin properly. Insulin is a hormone that helps glucose (sugar) enter your cells for energy. When insulin doesn't work well:

- Glucose builds up in your blood (high blood sugar)
- Your cells don't get the energy they need
- Over time, high blood sugar damages nerves, blood vessels, and organs

## How Is It Diagnosed?

| Test            | Normal      | Prediabetes | Diabetes              |
| --------------- | ----------- | ----------- | --------------------- |
| A1C             | < 5.7%      | 5.7-6.4%    | ≥ 6.5%                |
| Fasting glucose | < 100 mg/dL | 100-125     | ≥ 126                 |
| Random glucose  | < 140       | —           | ≥ 200 (with symptoms) |

:::info
**A1C** measures your average blood sugar over 2-3 months. It doesn't require fasting.
:::

## Risk Factors

### You Can Change

- Overweight or obesity (especially belly fat)
- Physical inactivity
- Unhealthy diet (high in processed foods, sugary drinks)
- Smoking

### You Can't Change

- Age 45+
- Family history (parent or sibling with type 2)
- Race/ethnicity (Black, Hispanic, Native American, Asian American, Pacific Islander higher risk)
- History of gestational diabetes
- PCOS (polycystic ovary syndrome)

## Managing Blood Sugar

### The ABCs of Diabetes

- **A** — **A1C** (target usually < 7%, individualized)
- **B** — **Blood pressure** (target < 130/80)
- **C** — **Cholesterol** (LDL < 70 or < 100 depending on risk)

### Food Choices

- **Carbohydrates** raise blood sugar the most. Choose:
  - Complex carbs: whole grains, beans, vegetables
  - Limit: white bread, pasta, rice, sweets, sugary drinks
- **Protein and healthy fats** have minimal effect on blood sugar
- **Fiber** slows sugar absorption — aim for 25-30g daily

:::success
The "plate method": 1/2 plate non-starchy vegetables, 1/4 lean protein, 1/4 complex carbs.
:::

### Physical Activity

- 150 minutes moderate activity per week
- **Resistance training** 2-3 times/week builds muscle, which uses glucose
- **Break up sitting** — stand or walk every 30 minutes

### Medications

Many options exist. Your doctor chooses based on:

- Your A1C
- Other health conditions
- Cost and insurance
- Side effect profile

| Class            | How It Works                          | Common Examples              |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Metformin        | Lowers liver glucose production       | First-line for most          |
| SGLT2 inhibitors | Excrete glucose in urine              | Empagliflozin, dapagliflozin |
| GLP-1 agonists   | Increase insulin, slow digestion      | Semaglutide, dulaglutide     |
| DPP-4 inhibitors | Increase insulin after meals          | Sitagliptin, linagliptin     |
| Sulfonylureas    | Stimulate pancreas to release insulin | Glipizide, glimepiride       |
| Insulin          | Replaces missing insulin              | Various types                |

:::warning
Some diabetes medications can cause **low blood sugar (hypoglycemia)**. Know the signs: shaky, sweaty, confused, fast heartbeat. Treat with 15g fast-acting carbs (juice, glucose tabs).
:::

## Monitoring Blood Sugar

- **A1C:** Every 3-6 months
- **Self-monitoring:** As directed (before meals, bedtime, or after meals)
- **Continuous glucose monitor (CGM):** Real-time readings, alerts for high/low

## Preventing Complications

High blood sugar over time damages:

- **Eyes** (retinopathy) — Yearly dilated eye exam
- **Kidneys** (nephropathy) — Yearly urine albumin test
- **Nerves** (neuropathy) — Yearly foot exam, daily foot checks
- **Heart and vessels** — Manage BP, cholesterol, don't smoke
- **Feet** — Poor circulation + nerve damage = infection risk

:::success
Good control dramatically reduces complication risk. Small improvements in A1C make a big difference.
:::

## When to Call Your Doctor

- Blood sugar consistently above target
- Symptoms of high blood sugar: extreme thirst, frequent urination, blurred vision, fatigue
- Symptoms of low blood sugar: shaky, sweaty, confused, dizzy
- Illness (blood sugar often rises when sick)
- New symptoms: foot sores, vision changes, numbness
