## Decision Matrix

| Situation                                         | Where to Go           | Why                                                  |
| ------------------------------------------------- | --------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Chest pain, difficulty breathing, stroke symptoms | **Call 911**          | These are emergencies that need treatment in minutes |
| Broken bone (no bone through skin)                | **Urgent care or ER** |                                                      |
| Fever 101°F+, feels awful but can walk/talk       | **Urgent care**       |                                                      |
| Fever 103°F+ or confusion                         | **ER**                | Could be serious infection                           |
| Deep cut with bleeding                            | **ER**                | May need stitches                                    |
| Skin infection (red, warm, swollen)               | **Urgent care**       |                                                      |
| Migraine, eye pain, vision changes                | **ER**                | Could be serious                                     |
| Small cut, mild cold, regular check-up            | **Doctor's office**   |                                                      |
| Allergic reaction with trouble breathing          | **Call 911**          |                                                      |

## Call 911 (Emergency) For

### Heart Attack Signs

- Chest discomfort (pressure, squeezing, fullness)
- Pain spreading to arms, back, neck, jaw, stomach
- Shortness of breath — with or without chest pain
- Cold sweat, nausea, lightheadedness

### Stroke Signs (BE FAST)

- **B**alance — sudden dizziness or loss of balance
- **E**yes — sudden vision changes or loss
- **F**ace drooping — one side doesn't move
- **A**rm weakness — one arm drifts down
- **S**peech difficulty — slurred or hard to understand
- **T**ime — call 911 immediately

### Other 911 Emergencies

- Difficulty breathing (not just a little winded)
- Severe allergic reaction (swelling face/lips, trouble breathing)
- Severe bleeding (blood spurting, can't stop with pressure)
- Seizure (especially first one, lasting >5 minutes, or in water)
- Head injury with confusion or passing out
- Poisoning or overdose
- Drowning or near-drowning
- Major burn (large area, deep, electrical, or to face/hands/genitals)
- Suicidal thoughts with plan or means

:::warning
If you're not sure if it's an emergency, **call 911 anyway.** Dispatchers can help assess. It's better to cancel an unnecessary ambulance than to delay emergency care.
:::

## Go to Emergency Room For

- Broken bone or dislocated joint
- Deep cut that may need stitches
- Severe abdominal pain
- Vomiting blood or blood in stool
- Severe burn
- Severe headache (worst of your life)
- Confusion or sudden behavior change
- Fever >103°F in adult (or >100.4°F in infant under 3 months)
- Dehydration (can't keep fluids down)
- Allergic reaction without breathing trouble but with hives/swelling
- Pregnancy complications (bleeding, severe pain)

## Go to Urgent Care For

- Cold, flu, sore throat, ear pain, sinus pain
- Minor cuts (may need stitches but not deep or bleeding badly)
- Sprains, strains, minor fractures (fingers, toes)
- Urinary tract infection (UTI)
- Mild to moderate allergic reaction (rash, hives)
- Pink eye (conjunctivitis)
- Minor burns (small, first-degree)
- Rash or skin infection (red, warm, but no fever, no spreading rapidly)
- Fever <103°F and otherwise OK

## Go to Your Doctor For

- Routine check-ups and physicals
- Ongoing management of chronic conditions
- Vaccines
- Prescription refills
- Non-urgent concerns (mild symptoms lasting weeks)
- Mental health concerns (therapy, medication management)

## What to Have Ready When You Call 911

- Your exact location (address, cross streets, building, floor, apartment)
- Phone number you're calling from
- What happened (brief description)
- Who is affected (age, sex, conscious? breathing?)
- Any known medical conditions, allergies, medications
- Don't hang up until dispatcher says to

:::info
Know your location even if traveling. Use What3Words app or share your phone's GPS coordinates.
:::

## What to Bring to the ER

- Photo ID and insurance card
- List of medications and doses
- List of allergies
- Emergency contact name and phone
- Phone charger
- Something for comfort (glasses, hearing aids, inhaler)
- Don't bring valuables or large amounts of cash

## Before You Go to Urgent Care

- Call ahead — some accept walk-ins, some prefer appointments
- Check if they accept your insurance
- Know their hours — many close by 9 PM
- Bring ID and insurance card

:::success
Most emergencies are not dramatic. Trust your gut. If something feels wrong or "off," seek care.
:::
