## Don't Panic — Get Context

Online lab portals often flag results in red before your doctor reviews them. A flagged result means "outside reference range" — not always "dangerous." Wait for your doctor's explanation unless you have urgent symptoms.

Learn which situations need fast action and which can wait for a scheduled follow-up.

## Results That Need Quick Action

Contact your doctor the same day (or seek emergency care if very ill) if results show:

- **Very high or very low blood sugar** — especially with confusion, vomiting, or extreme thirst
- **Critically high potassium** — can affect heart rhythm; may feel like weakness or palpitations
- **Severe anemia** — very low hemoglobin with shortness of breath, chest pain, or fainting
- **Signs of serious infection** — very high white blood cell count with fever and feeling severely ill
- **Acute kidney injury** — sharply rising creatinine with little urine output or swelling
- **Severe liver enzyme elevation** — with yellow skin/eyes, severe abdominal pain, or confusion

Your lab report may mark critical values as "panic" or "critical" — those require prompt medical contact.

:::warning
If you can't reach your doctor and feel very sick (trouble breathing, chest pain, confusion, severe weakness), go to urgent care or the emergency room.
:::

## Results to Monitor (Usually Not Emergencies)

These often need follow-up but rarely same-day emergency care:

- Mildly high cholesterol
- Prediabetes range A1C or glucose
- Slightly low or high thyroid levels
- Mild anemia without severe symptoms
- Borderline vitamin D or B12 levels
- Small changes from your previous results

Your doctor may recommend retesting in weeks or months, lifestyle changes, or starting treatment.

## When Symptoms Matter More Than Numbers

A "normal" lab value doesn't rule out illness if you feel unwell. Conversely, mild lab abnormalities in a healthy person may need only watching. Always report:

- New or worsening symptoms
- Medication changes
- Recent illness, surgery, or dehydration
- Pregnancy or major life stress

## Red Flags on the Lab Report

Look for notes like:

- **Critical / panic value** — call your doctor immediately
- **Hemolyzed sample** — test may need repeating
- **Insufficient sample** — redraw may be needed

If the lab says the sample was inadequate, results may not be reliable.

## Questions to Ask Your Doctor

When discussing abnormal results, ask:

- How serious is this result for me personally?
- What could be causing it?
- Do I need more tests before deciding treatment?
- What treatment options do I have?
- How will we monitor this over time?
- What lifestyle changes should I make now?
- What symptoms should prompt me to call sooner?

:::info
Bring prior lab results to appointments. A single high glucose matters less than a rising trend over three visits.
:::

:::success
Understanding urgency helps you respond calmly and appropriately — neither ignoring real problems nor losing sleep over minor variations.
:::
