## What Are Side Effects?

Side effects are unwanted reactions to a medication. They can range from mild (like a dry mouth) to severe (like an allergic reaction). Not everyone will experience side effects, and they may go away as your body gets used to the medicine.

## Common Mild Side Effects

These side effects are usually not serious and may go away on their own:

- Nausea or upset stomach
- Drowsiness or dizziness
- Dry mouth
- Headache
- Mild rash or itching

If these bother you, talk to your doctor or pharmacist. They may have suggestions to help.

## When to Call Your Doctor

Call your doctor right away if you experience:

- Trouble breathing or swallowing
- Severe rash or hives
- Swelling of the face, lips, or tongue
- Severe dizziness or fainting
- Chest pain or rapid heartbeat
- Severe stomach pain or vomiting
- Yellowing of the skin or eyes
- Unusual bleeding or bruising

:::warning
If you think you're having a serious reaction, call 911 or go to the emergency room.
:::
