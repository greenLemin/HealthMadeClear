## What Is Anxiety?

Anxiety is your body's natural alarm system. It prepares you for danger — but sometimes the alarm goes off when there's no threat. When anxiety is frequent, intense, or out of proportion to the situation, it may be an anxiety disorder.

## Normal Anxiety vs. Anxiety Disorder

| Normal Anxiety              | Anxiety Disorder                                 |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Temporary, passes quickly   | Persistent (weeks/months)                        |
| Proportionate to situation  | Out of proportion                                |
| Doesn't interfere with life | Interferes with work, relationships, daily tasks |
| You can redirect attention  | Hard to control or stop                          |

## Common Types of Anxiety Disorders

### Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

- Excessive worry about many things (health, money, family, work)
- Physical symptoms: muscle tension, fatigue, restlessness, trouble sleeping
- Difficulty controlling worry

### Panic Disorder

- Recurrent, unexpected panic attacks
- Fear of having another attack
- Avoidance of places where attacks occurred

### Social Anxiety Disorder

- Intense fear of social situations
- Fear of being judged, embarrassed, or humiliated
- Avoidance of social events, speaking up, eating in public

### Specific Phobias

- Intense fear of a specific object/situation (needles, heights, flying, enclosed spaces)
- Immediate anxiety response on exposure

## What Is a Panic Attack?

A panic attack is a sudden surge of intense fear that peaks within minutes. Symptoms include:

- Racing or pounding heart
- Sweating, trembling, shaking
- Shortness of breath or choking sensation
- Chest pain or discomfort
- Nausea or stomach distress
- Dizziness, lightheadedness, feeling faint
- Chills or heat sensations
- Numbness or tingling
- Feeling detached from yourself or reality
- Fear of losing control, "going crazy," or dying

:::warning
Panic attack symptoms can mimic a heart attack. If you're unsure, seek emergency care. Once diagnosed, you can learn to recognize them.
:::

## What Causes Anxiety Disorders?

- **Genetics:** 30-40% heritability
- **Brain chemistry:** Imbalance in serotonin, GABA, norepinephrine
- **Personality:** High neuroticism, behavioral inhibition
- **Trauma:** Childhood adversity, abuse, neglect
- **Stress:** Major life changes, chronic stress, financial pressure
- **Medical causes:** Thyroid problems, heart arrhythmia, caffeine, medications

## Managing Anxiety

### Grounding Techniques (for acute anxiety/panic)

1. **5-4-3-2-1:** Name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste
2. **Deep breathing:** Inhale 4 counts, hold 4, exhale 6 — activates parasympathetic nervous system
3. **Cold water:** Splash cold water on face — triggers diving reflex, slows heart rate
4. **Self-talk:** "This is uncomfortable but not dangerous. It will pass."

### Lifestyle

- **Limit caffeine:** Increases heart rate, mimics anxiety symptoms
- **Regular exercise:** Burns off stress hormones, produces endorphins
- **Sleep:** 7-9 hours — sleep deprivation worsens anxiety
- **Reduce alcohol:** Short-term relief, long-term worsening
- **Mindfulness meditation:** 10 minutes daily reduces amygdala reactivity

### Professional Treatments

- **CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy)** — Gold standard for all anxiety disorders. Teaches you to challenge anxious thoughts and face fears gradually
- **Exposure therapy** — Gradually and safely facing feared situations
- **Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)** — Accepts anxiety, commits to valued action
- **Medications:**
  - SSRIs/SNRIs (same as depression) — daily, long-term
  - Buspirone — for GAD
  - Benzodiazepines (e.g., clonazepam, lorazepam) — short-term or as-needed only

:::success
Anxiety disorders are highly treatable. 70-90% of people improve with evidence-based treatment. You don't have to live with constant fear.
:::

## When to Seek Professional Help

- Anxiety prevents you from doing things you want to do
- You avoid work, school, or social situations
- Anxiety has lasted months and isn't improving
- You rely on alcohol or drugs to cope
- You have thoughts of self-harm

Call **988** (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) if anxiety is overwhelming and you feel you can't cope.
