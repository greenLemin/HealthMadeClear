## Why Ask Questions?

Asking questions about your medications helps you understand what you're taking and why. It can prevent mistakes, side effects, and dangerous drug interactions. You have the right to know everything about your medicines.

## Essential Questions to Ask

When your doctor prescribes a new medication, ask these questions:

1. What is the name of the medicine?
2. What is it supposed to do?
3. How and when should I take it?
4. How long will I need to take it?
5. What side effects should I watch for?
6. Are there any foods or drinks I should avoid?
7. What should I do if I miss a dose?

## About Drug Interactions

Drug interactions happen when two or more medicines react with each other. Always tell your doctor and pharmacist about:

- All prescription medicines you take
- Over-the-counter medicines (like aspirin or cold medicine)
- Vitamins and supplements
- Herbal remedies
- Any allergies you have

:::warning
Keep a list of all your medicines and show it to every doctor you visit.
:::
