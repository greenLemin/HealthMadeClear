## Why Preparation Matters

Doctor visits are often short — sometimes only 15 to 20 minutes. Good preparation helps you use that time well. When you arrive organized, your doctor can focus on your health instead of gathering basic information. Preparation also reduces anxiety because you know what to expect.

## Gather Your Information

Before your appointment, collect these important items:

- Your insurance card and photo ID
- A list of all current medications (name, dose, how often you take it)
- Vitamins, supplements, and over-the-counter medicines
- Your medical history, if you're seeing a new doctor
- Previous test results or medical records
- A list of your symptoms and when they started
- Names and phone numbers of other doctors you see
- Any questions you want to ask

If you use a patient portal, log in before your visit. Download recent lab results or visit summaries so you can refer to them during the appointment.

## Write Down Your Symptoms

Be specific about your symptoms. Vague descriptions like "I don't feel right" make it harder for your doctor to help. For each symptom, note:

- When it started (date or "about two weeks ago")
- How often it happens (daily, weekly, only at night)
- What makes it better or worse (food, activity, rest)
- How severe it is on a scale of 1–10
- Any other symptoms that happen at the same time

Example: "Headache — started March 3, happens 3–4 times per week, worse in the morning, severity 6/10, sometimes with nausea."

## Prepare Your Questions

Write down your questions before your visit. Put the most important ones first in case time runs short. Good questions include:

- What could be causing my symptoms?
- What tests do I need, and what will they show?
- What are my treatment options, including pros and cons?
- What should I do if my symptoms get worse?
- When should I follow up with you?
- Are there lifestyle changes that could help?
- What warning signs mean I should call or go to the ER?

Bring a friend or family member if you want support taking notes or asking questions.

## Know Your Visit Type

Different visits need different preparation:

- **Annual checkup:** List health goals, family history updates, and screening questions.
- **New symptom:** Focus on symptom timeline and what you've already tried.
- **Follow-up:** Bring your health journal and note whether treatments helped.
- **Specialist:** Bring referral paperwork and records from your primary doctor.

:::info
Bring a notepad and pen (or use your phone) to write down what your doctor says. You can also ask for a printed after-visit summary from the front desk or patient portal.
:::

## The Day Before

- Confirm the appointment time and location.
- Check if you need to fast or stop certain medicines before tests.
- Lay out clothes that make exams easy (sleeves that roll up, comfortable shoes).
- Get a good night's sleep so you can think clearly.

:::success
Preparation is a skill. Each visit gets easier as you learn what works for you.
:::
