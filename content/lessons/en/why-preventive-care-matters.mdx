## What Is Preventive Care?

Preventive care means health services that help you stay healthy and catch problems early. Instead of waiting until you're sick, preventive care helps you:

- Find health issues before they become serious
- Get vaccines that protect you from diseases
- Learn healthy habits that prevent illness
- Save money by avoiding costly treatments later

## Types of Preventive Care

### Screening Tests

Screening tests check for diseases before you have symptoms. Common screenings include:

- Blood pressure checks
- Cholesterol tests
- Cancer screenings (mammograms, colonoscopies, Pap tests)
- Diabetes screening
- Depression screening

### Vaccines (Immunizations)

Vaccines protect you from serious diseases. They train your immune system to fight infections. Common adult vaccines:

- Flu shot (every year)
- Tdap (tetanus, diphtheria, pertussis) - every 10 years
- Shingles vaccine (age 50+)
- Pneumonia vaccine (age 65+)
- COVID-19 vaccines (as recommended)

### Wellness Visits

A wellness visit is a check-up focused on prevention. Your doctor will:

- Review your health history
- Check your vital signs
- Discuss which screenings you need
- Talk about healthy lifestyle choices
- Update your vaccines

:::info
Under the Affordable Care Act, most insurance plans must cover preventive services at no cost to you. This means no copay, no coinsurance, and no deductible for these services.
:::

## Why It Matters

Many serious diseases start silently. High blood pressure, high cholesterol, and early cancer often have no symptoms. By the time you feel sick, the disease may be harder to treat.

:::warning
Skipping preventive care does not save money. Treating advanced disease costs far more than preventing it.
:::

## Healthy Habits Are Prevention Too

Prevention isn't just medical visits. Daily choices matter:

- Eat a balanced diet with fruits, vegetables, and whole grains
- Move your body 150 minutes per week
- Get 7-9 hours of sleep
- Don't smoke; limit alcohol
- Manage stress

:::success
Small changes add up. A 10-minute walk daily is better than no walk at all.
:::

## Talk to Your Doctor

Ask your doctor:

- What screenings do I need for my age?
- Am I up to date on vaccines?
- What lifestyle changes would help me most?
- When should I come back?

## Overcoming Common Barriers

Many people skip preventive care because of cost worries, fear of bad news, lack of time, or transportation. If cost is a concern, ask your clinic about financial assistance and confirm which services are covered at no charge under your plan. If anxiety holds you back, bring a trusted friend and write questions ahead of time. Telehealth visits may cover some preventive discussions when an in-person exam is not required.

:::info
Preventive care is an investment. One caught early — high blood pressure, prediabetes, or a precancerous polyp — can prevent a medical crisis years later.
:::
