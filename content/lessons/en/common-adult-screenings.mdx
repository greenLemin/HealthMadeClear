## Why Screening Schedules Vary

Screening recommendations depend on:

- Age
- Sex assigned at birth
- Family history
- Personal health history
- Lifestyle factors (smoking, weight, etc.)

The guidelines below are from the U.S. Preventive Services Task Force (USPSTF). **Always discuss with your doctor** — they may recommend different timing based on your situation.

## Ages 18-39

| Screening         | Frequency       | Notes                                     |
| ----------------- | --------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Blood pressure    | Every 1-2 years | More often if high                        |
| Cholesterol       | Every 4-6 years | Start at 20 if risk factors               |
| Skin cancer check | Talk to doctor  | Especially if fair skin or family history |
| STI screening     | As recommended  | Based on sexual activity                  |
| Dental exam       | Every 6 months  |                                           |
| Eye exam          | Every 2-4 years |                                           |

:::info
Women should also discuss: Pap test (cervical cancer) starting at 21, clinical breast exam, and chlamydia/gonorrhea screening if sexually active.
:::

## Ages 40-49

| Screening          | Frequency           | Notes                                     |
| ------------------ | ------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Blood pressure     | Every year          |                                           |
| Cholesterol        | Every 4-6 years     | More often if risk factors                |
| Diabetes screening | Every 3 years       | Start at 35 if overweight                 |
| Colorectal cancer  | **Start at 45**     | Colonoscopy every 10 years or other tests |
| Mammogram (women)  | Every 1-2 years     | Talk to doctor about starting at 40       |
| Skin cancer check  | Yearly if high risk |                                           |
| Dental exam        | Every 6 months      |                                           |
| Eye exam           | Every 2-4 years     |                                           |

:::warning
Colorectal cancer screening now starts at **age 45** (changed from 50 in 2021). Don't delay — early detection saves lives.
:::

| --------------------- | -------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Blood pressure        | Every year                       |                                                                                                  |
| Cholesterol           | Every 4-6 years                  |                                                                                                  |
| Diabetes              | Every 3 years                    |                                                                                                  |
| Colorectal cancer     | Every 10 years (colonoscopy)     | Or yearly FIT test                                                                               |
| Mammogram (women)     | Every 1-2 years                  |                                                                                                  |

| Eye exam              | Every 1-2 years                  |                                                                                                  |

## Ages 65+

| Screening            | Frequency        | Notes                                                                                                                 |
| -------------------- | ---------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Blood pressure       | Every year       |                                                                                                                       |
| Cholesterol          | Every 4-6 years  |                                                                                                                       |
| Colorectal cancer    | Until age 75     | Discuss after 75                                                                                                      |
| Mammogram (women)    | Every 1-2 years  | Until 74, then discuss                                                                                                |
| Lung cancer (CT)     | Yearly           | Until 80 if currently smoking or quit within the last 15 years with 20 pack-year history                              |
| Osteoporosis         | Every 2 years    |                                                                                                                       |
| Fall risk assessment | Yearly           |                                                                                                                       |
| Cognitive screening  | Yearly           |                                                                                                                       |
| Pneumonia vaccine    | One or two doses | Age 65+, or earlier with risk factors (ask your doctor or pharmacist which specific vaccine product is right for you) |
| Flu vaccine          | Every year       |                                                                                                                       |
| Dental exam          | Every 6 months   |                                                                                                                       |
| Eye exam             | Every year       |                                                                                                                       |

## How to Prepare for Screenings

1. **Know your family history** — Write down what conditions run in your family
2. **Bring your records** — Previous screening results, vaccine records
3. **Ask about fasting** — Some blood tests require 8-12 hours without food
4. **Wear comfortable clothes** — Easy to remove for exams
5. **Write down questions** — Don't rely on memory

:::success
Put screening reminders on your calendar. Treat them like any other important appointment.
:::
