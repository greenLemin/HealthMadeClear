## Review Your Instructions

As soon as you get home, review what your doctor told you while it's still fresh. Make sure you understand:

- What medicines to take, how much, and at what times
- Whether to take medicine with food or on an empty stomach
- Any lifestyle changes (diet, exercise, rest, work limits)
- When to schedule follow-up appointments or tests
- What to do if your symptoms get worse
- How long you should expect before feeling improvement

If anything is unclear, call the clinic's nurse line the same day. Don't guess about medications.

## Fill Your Prescriptions

If your doctor prescribed medication:

- Go to your pharmacy promptly so treatment isn't delayed
- Show the pharmacist your insurance card
- Ask the pharmacist to review how to take the medicine
- Confirm whether this drug interacts with your other medicines
- Read the label carefully before taking any dose
- Store medicine as directed (some need refrigeration)

Use one pharmacy when possible so they have a complete record of your prescriptions.

## Schedule Follow-Up Care

Don't wait until you feel sick again to book your next visit. Schedule:

- Follow-up appointments your doctor recommended
- Lab work or imaging before your next visit
- Specialist referrals (call within a few days)
- Screening tests you're due for (mammogram, colonoscopy, etc.)

Put reminders on your calendar and set phone alerts for medication times.

## Track Your Progress

Keep a simple health journal — paper or phone app — to track:

- Symptoms and how they change day to day
- Side effects from new medications
- Blood pressure, blood sugar, or weight if your doctor asked you to monitor
- Questions that come up between visits
- Improvements or worsening of your condition

Bring your journal to your next appointment. Patterns help your doctor adjust treatment.

## Handle Test Results

If tests were ordered:

- Ask when and how you'll get results (portal, phone, mail)
- Check your patient portal if you have one
- Don't panic over numbers you don't understand — wait for your doctor's explanation
- Call if you haven't heard back by the promised date

:::info
Bring your health journal to your next appointment to help your doctor understand your progress.
:::

## Know When to Call Back

Contact your doctor's office if you:

- Have side effects that bother you or worry you
- Can't afford a prescribed medicine (alternatives may exist)
- Symptoms worsen instead of improve
- Miss a dose and aren't sure what to do
- Need a refill before your next visit

:::warning
Call 911 or go to the emergency room for chest pain, trouble breathing, severe allergic reactions, sudden weakness, or other emergency symptoms — don't wait for a callback.
:::
