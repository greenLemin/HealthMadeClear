## Why Sleep Matters

Sleep is essential for physical and mental health. During sleep, your body:

- Repairs muscle and tissue
- Consolidates memories and learning
- Regulates mood and emotions
- Supports immune function
- Manages hunger and metabolism hormones

## How Much Sleep Do You Need?

| Age             | Recommended Hours |
| --------------- | ----------------- |
| Adults 18-60    | 7+ hours          |
| Adults 61-64    | 7-9 hours         |
| Adults 65+      | 7-8 hours         |
| Teenagers 14-17 | 8-10 hours        |
| Children 6-12   | 9-12 hours        |

## What Happens When You Don't Get Enough Sleep?

### Short-term effects (1-2 nights):

- Irritability and mood swings
- Difficulty concentrating and making decisions
- Slower reaction time (similar to alcohol impairment)
- Increased appetite, especially for high-sugar foods

### Long-term effects (chronic sleep loss):

- Higher risk: obesity, diabetes, heart disease, high blood pressure
- Weakened immune system (more colds, slower recovery)
- Increased risk of depression and anxiety
- Memory problems and cognitive decline
- Reduced life expectancy

:::warning
Driving while sleep-deprived is as dangerous as driving drunk. After 18+ hours awake, your impairment equals a BAC of 0.05%.
:::

## Common Sleep Disorders

### Insomnia

- Difficulty falling asleep, staying asleep, or waking too early
- Affects 30% of adults (10% chronic)
- Treatment: CBT-I (cognitive behavioral therapy for insomnia) is first-line

### Sleep Apnea

- Breathing stops and starts during sleep
- Loud snoring, gasping, choking sounds
- Daytime sleepiness despite "sleeping through the night"
- Increases heart disease and stroke risk
- Treatment: CPAP machine, oral appliance, weight loss

### Restless Legs Syndrome (RLS)

- Uncontrollable urge to move legs, especially at rest
- Worse at night — disrupts sleep
- Treatment: Iron supplements (if low), medications, lifestyle changes

## Sleep Hygiene: Your Daily Habits

### Do

- **Wake up same time daily** — most important for regulating circadian rhythm
- **Get morning sunlight** — 10-15 minutes within 1 hour of waking
- **Exercise regularly** — but not within 2-3 hours of bedtime
- **Create a wind-down routine** — 30-60 minutes before bed (read, stretch, warm bath)
- **Use bed only for sleep and sex** — trains brain to associate bed with sleep
- **Keep bedroom cool** — 65-68°F optimal

### Don't

- **No screens before bed** — blue light suppresses melatonin, content stimulates brain
- **No caffeine after noon** — half-life is 5-6 hours
- **No alcohol within 3 hours of bed** — disrupts REM sleep, causes night waking
- **No large meals within 2-3 hours** — digestion interferes with sleep
- **No clock-watching** — anxiety about time makes insomnia worse

:::info
CBT-I (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia) is the most effective treatment for chronic insomnia — more effective than sleep medications long-term.
:::

## When to See a Doctor About Sleep

- Trouble sleeping 3+ nights/week for 3+ months
- Loud snoring with gasping or choking sounds
- Falling asleep during daytime activities (driving, working)
- Leg sensations that prevent sleep
- Awake feeling unrefreshed despite 7-8 hours in bed

## Common Sleep Myths

| Myth                                    | Truth                                                         |
| --------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "You can catch up on sleep on weekends" | Partial recovery, but circadian disruption remains            |
| "Older people need less sleep"          | Need stays 7-8 hours; ability to get it often changes         |
| "Alcohol helps you sleep better"        | Helps fall asleep faster but ruins sleep quality              |
| "Your body adapts to less sleep"        | No — performance declines even if you feel fine               |
| "Melatonin fixes all sleep problems"    | Good for jet lag/shift work; limited use for chronic insomnia |

:::success
Better sleep is achievable. Start with one change — consistent wake time — and build from there.
:::
