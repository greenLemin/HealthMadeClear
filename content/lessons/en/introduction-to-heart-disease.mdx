## What Is Heart Disease?

Heart disease (cardiovascular disease) refers to several conditions affecting the heart and blood vessels. The most common type is **coronary artery disease (CAD)** — when plaque builds up in the arteries supplying the heart.

## Main Types of Heart Disease

### Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)

Plaque (cholesterol, fat, calcium) narrows coronary arteries. Reduces blood flow to heart muscle.

- **Angina:** Chest pain/discomfort from reduced blood flow
- **Heart attack:** Blood flow completely blocked — heart muscle dies

### Heart Failure

Heart can't pump enough blood to meet body's needs. Doesn't mean heart "stops" — means it's not working efficiently.

- Symptoms: shortness of breath, fatigue, swelling in legs/ankles, rapid weight gain

### Arrhythmia

Irregular heartbeat — too fast, too slow, or irregular.

- **Atrial fibrillation (AFib):** Most common; increases stroke risk 5x
- Symptoms: palpitations, dizziness, fatigue, shortness of breath

### Valve Disease

Heart valves don't open or close properly. Can be present at birth or develop later.

## Major Risk Factors

### Modifiable (You Can Change)

- **High blood pressure** — #1 risk factor
- **High LDL cholesterol** — builds plaque
- **Diabetes** — damages blood vessels
- **Smoking** — damages vessels, raises BP, lowers oxygen
- **Obesity** — especially belly fat
- **Physical inactivity**
- **Unhealthy diet** — high sodium, saturated fat, added sugar
- **Excessive alcohol**
- **Stress and poor sleep**

### Non-Modifiable

- Age (men 45+, women 55+)
- Family history of early heart disease
- Sex (men higher risk earlier; women catch up after menopause)
- Race/ethnicity (Black adults higher rates)

## Warning Signs of Heart Attack

| Symptom                | Description                                                                                  |
| ---------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Chest discomfort       | Pressure, squeezing, fullness, pain in center/left chest — lasts > few minutes or comes/goes |
| Upper body pain        | Arms, back, neck, jaw, stomach                                                               |
| Shortness of breath    | With or without chest discomfort                                                             |
| Cold sweat             | Unexplained sweating                                                                         |
| Nausea/lightheadedness | Especially in women                                                                          |

:::warning
**Women often have different symptoms:** unusual fatigue, sleep disturbances, indigestion, anxiety, shortness of breath without chest pain. Don't ignore subtle signs.
:::

## Warning Signs of Stroke (BE FAST)

- **B**alance — sudden loss
- **E**yes — vision changes
- **F**ace — drooping on one side
- **A**rm — weakness/numbness
- **S**peech — slurred or confused
- **T**ime — call 911 immediately

## Protecting Your Heart

### Life's Essential 8 (American Heart Association)

1. **Eat better** — Mediterranean or DASH diet
2. **Be active** — 150 min moderate or 75 min vigorous/week
3. **Quit tobacco** — including vaping
4. **Get healthy sleep** — 7-9 hours/night
5. **Manage weight** — BMI 18.5-24.9
6. **Control cholesterol** — LDL < 100 (or < 70 if high risk)
7. **Manage blood sugar** — A1C < 5.7%
8. **Manage blood pressure** — < 120/80

:::success
You don't have to be perfect. Every healthy choice adds up. Progress, not perfection.
:::

### Heart-Healthy Eating

- **More:** vegetables, fruits, whole grains, beans, nuts, fish, olive oil
- **Less:** red meat, processed meat, refined grains, added sugar, sodium
- **Limit saturated fat** to < 10% of calories
- **No trans fats** (partially hydrogenated oils)

### Know Your Numbers (Get Checked)

| Test              | Target                    |
| ----------------- | ------------------------- |
| Blood pressure    | < 120/80                  |
| Total cholesterol | < 200 mg/dL               |
| LDL ("bad")       | < 100 (or < 70 high risk) |
| HDL ("good")      | > 40 men, > 50 women      |
| Triglycerides     | < 150                     |
| Fasting glucose   | < 100                     |
| A1C               | < 5.7%                    |

## When to Call Your Doctor

- New or worsening chest discomfort
- Shortness of breath with activity or at rest
- Irregular heartbeat (fluttering, racing, skipping)
- Swelling in legs, ankles, feet
- Unexplained fatigue or inability to do usual activities
- Dizziness, fainting, or near-fainting
